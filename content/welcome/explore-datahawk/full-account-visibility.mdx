DataHawk gives leadership, ecommerce, finance, and operations teams one connected view of <Term>marketplace</Term> performance. You can see what is growing, what is losing momentum, and which accounts, products, brands, or markets need attention.

## What you can achieve with DataHawk

- See sales, traffic, profit, inventory, and advertising performance in one place.
- Compare Seller, Vendor, Advertising, and marketplace data without rebuilding exports by hand.
- Identify which products are driving revenue, margin, traffic, or operational risk.
- Understand whether growth is profitable after Amazon fees, returns, advertising spend, and uploaded <Term>COGS</Term>.
- Spot disconnects between account-level performance and product-level performance before they become planning mistakes.

## Business outcomes

| Outcome | How DataHawk helps |
|---|---|
| Know what changed | Dashboards surface revenue, traffic, inventory, ad, and profit movement by product, brand, account, and marketplace |
| Protect margin | P&L views connect revenue with fees, COGS, returns, and marketing spend |
| Prioritize action | Product-level views show where performance drops, stock risk, or fee burden deserve attention first |
| Align teams | Finance, operations, ecommerce, and advertising teams work from the same refreshed data foundation |

## Where this creates value

For executives, DataHawk turns disconnected marketplace signals into an account-level performance story. For operators, it narrows the weekly question from "what happened?" to "which products do we fix first?"

You can start with pre-built dashboards, then go deeper through <Term>Snowflake</Term>, <Term>BigQuery</Term>, Power BI, Looker Studio, Excel, Google Sheets, or your own reporting stack.
