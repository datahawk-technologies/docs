DataHawk connects to your Amazon and Walmart accounts and structures your data so you can answer the questions that matter to your business — without building a data pipeline or pulling reports manually.

Below is a sample of the questions DataHawk helps teams answer every day, organised by role.

## For Amazon sellers (Seller Central)

**Sales & revenue**

**Why did my revenue drop this week, and which products drove it?**
You open your dashboard on Monday morning and revenue is down 18% week over week. DataHawk's Seller Analytics Dashboard shows you immediately that three ASINs account for 80% of the drop — two lost the Buy Box over the weekend, and one had a sharp decline in sessions. You know exactly where to focus before the day starts.

**How does my sales performance compare to the same period last year?**
Before your quarterly review, you pull up the year-over-year comparison in DataHawk. Revenue is up 12% overall, but two of your top SKUs are flat while a newer product is driving most of the growth — insight that changes how you present the story to your leadership team.

**What is my average selling price and order value, and how are they trending?**
You recently ran a promotional discount and want to know if it moved volume without eroding too much margin. DataHawk shows your ASP trending down 6% over the promotion period alongside a 22% uplift in units — giving you the data to decide whether the trade-off was worth it.

**Which products are my top and bottom revenue contributors this month?**
You manage a catalogue of 80 SKUs and need to decide where to invest in restocking. DataHawk ranks every product by revenue contribution and flags which are trending up versus down — so your restock decisions are based on data, not gut feel.

---

**Profitability**

**What is my actual profit after Amazon fees, advertising spend, and returns?**
Your revenue looks healthy, but you suspect fees are eating into margins. DataHawk's P&L view combines your Amazon fees, ad spend, returns, and your uploaded COGS into a single profit view — showing you that your net margin is 11%, not the 22% gross revenue implied.

**How much of my revenue is consumed by FBA fulfilment and referral fees?**
You're comparing FBA versus FBM for a high-volume product. DataHawk breaks out every fee category by ASIN, so you can see the exact fee burden per unit and run a proper comparison before making the switch.

**Which products have the healthiest and weakest margins?**
Before planning your next advertising push, you check which products actually have margin to absorb ad spend. DataHawk surfaces your top and bottom performers by net margin — so you invest behind products that can generate real profit, not just revenue.

**How has my net profit margin evolved over the last 12 months?**
You want to show investors that the business is becoming more efficient. DataHawk's trend view shows net margin month by month, so you can see the impact of fee changes, pricing adjustments, and cost improvements over time — not just a snapshot.

---

**Advertising**

**Which campaigns have the highest ACoS and need to be cut or optimised?**
You're reviewing this month's spend and your overall ACoS is up 8 points. DataHawk's Advertising Dashboard shows you the exact campaigns driving the spike — broken down by campaign type, product, and keyword — so you can pause or adjust bids before more budget is wasted.

**What is my total advertising spend as a percentage of all my sales — not just ad-attributed sales (TACoS)?**
Your ACoS looks fine at 18%, but you suspect ad spend is a larger share of your business than it appears. DataHawk calculates TACoS across your full revenue base, revealing that advertising is actually consuming 28% of total sales — a number that reframes your efficiency conversation entirely.

**Which products are generating ad-attributed revenue, and at what efficiency?**
You've been running ads across 40 products but want to know which ones are actually pulling their weight. DataHawk shows ad-attributed revenue and ACoS by ASIN, so you can concentrate budget on products with strong conversion and pull back on those that are burning spend without returning sales.

**How is my advertising performance trending week over week?**
You changed your bidding strategy two weeks ago and want to know if it's working. DataHawk's week-over-week view shows spend, ACoS, and ROAS trending together — giving you enough signal to know whether to hold the course or revert.

---

**Traffic & listings**

**How are my page views and session conversion rates trending?**
Traffic is up but orders are flat — something is breaking in the middle. DataHawk surfaces your session conversion rate by ASIN over time, so you can identify exactly which products have a growing traffic gap and prioritise listing improvements where they'll have the most impact.

**Am I winning the Buy Box on my key products, and who is winning it when I'm not?**
You've noticed a revenue dip on one of your top sellers but the listing looks fine. DataHawk shows Buy Box ownership percentage over time and flags when you lost it — so you can investigate pricing, fulfilment, or seller competition as the likely cause.

**Which products have poor listing quality scores (LQS) that may be hurting conversion?**
Before a peak sales period, you want to make sure your catalogue is in good shape. DataHawk's LQS view flags which ASINs are missing images, have thin bullet points, or fall below quality thresholds — so you can fix the ones most likely to be losing you conversions.

---

**Inventory**

**Which products are at risk of going out of stock based on current sales velocity?**
You're three weeks out from a peak period and need to know where your exposure is. DataHawk calculates days of supply for every ASIN based on recent sales velocity, so you can see immediately which products will run out before your next shipment arrives.

**How many days of supply do I have for each ASIN?**
Before a restocking call with your 3PL, you pull DataHawk's inventory snapshot. Every ASIN shows current FBA stock and days of supply at current run rate — giving you a clear, prioritised list to take into the conversation.

**What should I reorder now, and how much, based on my lead time and target stock cover?**
You manage a catalogue with varying lead times and don't want to over-order. DataHawk's reorder recommendations factor in your sales velocity, current stock, and target cover period — outputting a quantity and timing recommendation for each ASIN.

## For Amazon vendors (Vendor Central)

**Sales & traffic**

**How are my glance views and conversion rate trending versus last year?**
Category traffic was reportedly up this quarter, but your glance views are flat. DataHawk shows the year-over-year comparison for every ASIN, revealing that while category visits grew, your detail page share declined — a signal that competitors are gaining visibility at your expense.

**What is the gap between ordered revenue and shipped revenue, and what is causing it?**
Your P&L shows a variance between orders placed and revenue recognised, and finance wants an explanation. DataHawk shows ordered versus shipped revenue by ASIN and week, so you can trace whether the gap is a fulfilment lag, a cancellation pattern, or a demand forecasting issue.

**Which products are generating the most traffic, and which are losing it?**
Ahead of your next range review with Amazon, you want to know which products are gaining traction and which are fading. DataHawk ranks your catalogue by glance view trend, so you can build the conversation around data rather than anecdote.

---

**Inventory & supply chain**

**Which products are at risk of going out of stock based on Amazon's demand forecast?**
Amazon's purchasing has slowed on two of your core SKUs and you want to understand the risk. DataHawk surfaces Amazon's out-of-stock probability and weeks of cover for each ASIN, so you can flag the issue to your vendor manager before it becomes a lost-sale event.

**What is my purchase order confirmation rate and fill rate — and where are the gaps?**
Your ops team suspects fill rate issues are straining the Amazon relationship. DataHawk tracks PO confirmation and fill rate over time, by product and by marketplace, so you can quantify the problem and identify whether it's a specific SKU, warehouse, or seasonal pattern.

**How long is my average vendor lead time, and how does it vary by marketplace?**
You're trying to improve your PO acceptance SLA and need a baseline. DataHawk pulls historical lead time data by marketplace and product category, so you can see where your performance is strong and where there's room to negotiate or improve.

---

**Advertising**

**What is my TACoS (total ad spend as % of shipped revenue)?**
Your advertising team reports a healthy ACoS, but the CFO wants to know what advertising actually costs relative to total business. DataHawk calculates TACoS against shipped revenue, giving both teams a shared number to work from in budget planning discussions.

**Which sponsored types (SP, SB, SD) are delivering the best return?**
You're allocating next quarter's ad budget and need to know which formats are working. DataHawk breaks down spend, attributed revenue, and ROAS by sponsored type, so you can shift budget toward what's delivering and reduce waste on formats that aren't.

**How does my product-level ad performance break down by brand or tag?**
You manage advertising across three brands in the same vendor account and need to see performance separately. DataHawk's tagging and filtering lets you slice ad performance by brand, so each brand manager sees only their numbers and you can report at the portfolio level too.

---

**Market position**

**How is my brand's share of voice evolving for the keywords I'm tracking?**
You ran a Share of Voice push over Q4 and want to know if it moved the needle. DataHawk tracks your brand's organic and sponsored visibility across your keyword set over time — so you can see whether you gained ground, held steady, or gave up position to a competitor.

**Which competitors are gaining organic visibility at my expense?**
A key product's BSR has been slipping for three months. DataHawk's keyword rank tracking shows which brand moved up on the terms where you moved down — giving you a named competitor to investigate and a set of keywords to prioritise in your next listing and ad optimisation.

## For advertising managers

**Which campaigns have the worst ACoS, and what is driving it — spend or weak conversion?**
Two campaigns are dragging your portfolio ACoS above target, but you need to know whether to cut spend or fix the product page. DataHawk breaks down each campaign's performance by spend efficiency and conversion rate separately — so you can distinguish a bidding problem from a listing problem before making changes.

**Which keywords are generating clicks but not converting to orders?**
You're spending on hundreds of keywords and suspect a significant portion are wasted. DataHawk surfaces your highest-spend, lowest-converting keywords in a single view — so you can pause or reduce bids on the ones burning budget without returning revenue.

**How do my Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display campaigns compare in efficiency?**
Before the next budget cycle, you need to justify how spend is allocated across campaign types. DataHawk's side-by-side comparison shows ROAS, ACoS, and conversion rate by sponsored type — giving you a data-backed argument for where to invest more and where to cut.

**Which products are generating the most ad-attributed revenue?**
You're running ads across a large catalogue and want to identify your advertising anchors — the products where ads genuinely drive business. DataHawk ranks products by ad-attributed revenue and efficiency, so you can protect those budgets and rethink the rest.

**What is the split of my budget across campaign types, and is it aligned with performance?**
Your gut says too much is going to Sponsored Display, but you need the numbers. DataHawk shows your budget split and performance by campaign type in the same view, so you can see immediately whether the allocation matches where the returns are coming from.

**How does my advertising performance compare month over month and year over year?**
You're preparing a performance review and need to show whether the advertising strategy is improving over time. DataHawk's trend view lets you overlay spend, ACoS, and revenue month by month and versus the prior year — so the narrative is grounded in numbers, not impressions.

**Which search terms are converting well that I should add as exact-match keywords?**
You've been running broad and phrase match campaigns and want to harvest what's working. DataHawk surfaces your highest-converting search terms ranked by orders and conversion rate — giving you a ready-made list to review for exact-match or product targeting expansion.

**How are my DSP campaigns performing in terms of new-to-brand sales and detail page views?**
Your DSP spend is significant and the brand team wants to know what it's actually buying. DataHawk's DSP reporting breaks out new-to-brand orders, detail page views, and branded search lift — so you can demonstrate the upper-funnel impact beyond last-click attribution.

## For market & category intelligence

**Who are the top brands in my category, and what is their estimated market share?**
Before a board presentation, you need to show where your brand stands in a competitive category. DataHawk's Market Intelligence Dashboard ranks the top brands by estimated revenue share and BSR performance — so you can present a fact-based market map, not an estimate.

**Is my brand gaining or losing market share over the last 6 to 12 months?**
You've grown revenue 15% this year, but you suspect the category grew faster. DataHawk tracks your share of the Top 100 ASINs over time, so you can see whether your growth represents real market share gain or simply riding a rising tide.

**What is the typical price range for the best-selling products in my category?**
You're launching a new product and want to price it competitively. DataHawk shows the price distribution across the Top 100 in your category — so you can see where the volume is concentrated and where premium or value positioning creates an opening.

**Which brands are consistently present in the Top 100, and which are rising or falling?**
You want to know which competitors are entrenched and which are vulnerable. DataHawk tracks brand presence in the category Top 100 week by week, so you can see who has consistent staying power and who is gaining or losing ground across a rolling period.

**Are there new brands gaining traction in my category that I should be monitoring?**
A new entrant appeared in your category last quarter and seems to be gaining reviews quickly. DataHawk surfaces emerging brands by BSR movement and review velocity — so you can start tracking them early, before they become a significant threat.

**How does my Best Sellers Rank compare to competitors, and how has it moved over time?**
You're preparing a competitive briefing and need to show relative positioning over time, not just a snapshot. DataHawk's BSR trend view lets you compare your key ASINs against named competitors across a 12-month window — giving you a clear picture of momentum, not just rank.

## For finance & operations teams

**What is our gross profit, operating profit, and net profit — broken down by product, brand, or marketplace?**
Finance needs a P&L by marketplace for the quarterly close and the data is scattered across Seller Central reports. DataHawk consolidates revenue, fees, COGS, and ad spend into a single structured P&L — queryable by product, brand, or marketplace — so the monthly close goes from days to hours.

**What is our total Amazon fee burden across the portfolio?**
Amazon fees have increased twice this year and you need to quantify the impact. DataHawk breaks out every fee type — FBA fulfilment, referral, storage, returns — by ASIN and rolled up to portfolio level, so you can model the exact cost and present a clear number to your CFO.

**Which products have the highest return rates, and what is the financial impact?**
Returns are running higher than expected and you need to find the source. DataHawk shows return rate by ASIN alongside the revenue impact, so you can quickly identify whether the issue is concentrated in a specific product, category, or time period — and quantify what fixing it is worth.

**How is our profitability trending across different selling periods?**
The business is seasonal and you need to understand whether peak periods are genuinely profitable once fees and ad spend are included. DataHawk's P&L trend view overlays profitability across selling periods — so you can see whether Q4 volume actually translates to Q4 profit.

**Where are our COGS-to-revenue ratios out of line with targets?**
Your category manager suspects a product line is underperforming on margin but doesn't have the data to prove it. DataHawk compares COGS to revenue by product and flags where the ratio sits outside target thresholds — giving the finance and commercial teams a shared fact base to work from.

# Pricing

## How pricing works

DataHawk pricing has three components:

1. **Platform fee** 

A flat base fee representing the number of credit you need for using the database, the standard amount is 20.

1. **Account Analytics**

Based on your data volume:

- **For Sellers accounts:** proxied by number of units sold or orders
- **For Vendors accounts:** proxied by the catalog size
- **For Advertising accounts:** proxied by the amount of ad spend

**2. Digital Shelf Analytics**

Based on the number of items you track:

- 1 product /Market (1 ASIN tracking on US, FR, and UK = 3 product tracked)
- keyword /Market
- category /Market

Volume discounts apply based on total contract size.

## What does tracking include?

| What you track | What data you get |
| --- | --- |
| **Product** | Listing details, sales ranks, attributes, pricing, offers, Buy Box, rating, and rating count |
| **Keyword** | Organic ranks and SEO visibility |
| **Category** | Top 100 products, market share, brand performance |

**Note:** Product tracking does not include keyword/SEO data — that requires separate keyword tracking.
