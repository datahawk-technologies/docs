DataHawk's Seller + Vendor Analytics Dashboard consolidates reporting across both Seller Central (<Term>3P</Term>) and Vendor Central (<Term>1P</Term>) accounts into a single executive view. It covers traffic, sales, advertising, and inventory, with unified metrics designed for brands operating on both channels simultaneously. Because the revenue models differ structurally — on the Vendor side, earned revenue (Shipped COGS) differs from consumer demand (Ordered Revenue), while on the Seller side these are the same figure — all combined metrics are clearly labelled to reflect which revenue base they use.

## Questions this dashboard helps answer

- What is my total business performance across both Seller Central and Vendor Central?
- How is revenue split between my <Term>1P</Term> and <Term>3P</Term> channels?
- How does consumer demand (Ordered Revenue) compare to my earned revenue (Shipped <Term>COGS</Term>) on the Vendor side?
- How efficient is my advertising spend across both channels combined?
- What is the profitability breakdown for each channel after fees, <Term>COGS</Term>, and advertising?
- Which products are the top performers across Seller and Vendor?
- What is my current <Term>FBA</Term> inventory status and when do I need to replenish?
- How does my Vendor inventory cover projected demand, and what is Amazon's restocking forecast?
- How has my blended average selling price evolved across both channels?

## Before you start

**What you need:**

- [Seller Central account](/help-center/data-setup/seller-account) connected in DataHawk
- [Vendor Central account](/help-center/data-setup/vendor-account) connected in DataHawk
- [Advertising account](/help-center/data-setup/ads-account) connected in DataHawk (Seller and/or Vendor)
- <Term>COGS</Term> uploaded to DataHawk (required for P&L tabs — see [Upload Product Expenses](/help-center/data-setup/cogs-amz-upload))

**Set up:** Download and configure the Power BI template. See [Connect to Power BI](/help-center/connect-tools/powerbi).

**Data history:** The Inventory Timeline tab covers the past 30 days only. All other tabs support the full historical range available in your DataHawk workspace.

## Dashboard tabs at a glance

| Tab | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Overview** | Combined Seller + Vendor executive summary with blended revenue, units, page views, and advertising metrics |
| **Sales (Seller)** | Seller Central sales performance: orders, units, <Term>ASP</Term>, AOV, page views, and conversion |
| **Sales (Vendor)** | Vendor Central sales performance: Shipped <Term>COGS</Term>, Ordered Revenue, Glance Views, and conversion |
| **Advertising** | Consolidated advertising performance across <Term>SP</Term>, <Term>SB</Term>, and <Term>SD</Term> for both channels |
| **P&L (Seller)** | Full profit and loss for Seller Central — gross revenue through net profit |
| **P&L (Vendor)** | Full profit and loss for Vendor Central — shipped revenue through net profit |
| **Inventory Timeline** | 30-day <Term>FBA</Term> inventory history across all fulfillment stages (Seller) |
| **FBA Recommendations** | Amazon's restock suggestions and custom replenishment calculations (Seller) |
| **Inventory Snapshot** | Vendor inventory health, aging, purchase orders, and Amazon demand forecast |
| **Explorer** | Custom cross-metric analysis with any two metrics plotted over a configurable timeline |
| **Download Center** | Build and export custom data tables by selecting rows, columns, and metrics |

## Dashboard filters

Global slicers apply to all pages unless a page has its own local slicer, in which case the local slicer takes precedence.

### Date filters

| Filter | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Date Range** | Calendar picker for start and end dates |
| **Granularity** | Year / Quarter / Month / Week / Day |
| **Period comparison** | Toggle between PoP (period over period) and YoY (year over year) |

### Scope filters

| Filter | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Marketplace** | Filter by Amazon country (US, CA, UK, DE, etc.) |
| **Seller Account** | Filter by connected Seller Central account |
| **Vendor Account** | Filter by connected Vendor Central account |
| **Brand** | Filter by brand name |
| **Tags** | Filter by custom <Term>ASIN</Term> tags created in DataHawk |
| **ASIN** | Filter by specific ASINs |
| **SKU** | Filter by specific Seller <Term>SKU</Term>s |

## For analysts & developers

### Glossary

**Navigation**

- **bps** — absolute change in percentage points. 100 bps = 1 percentage point.
- **PoP** — period over period. Relative difference between two consecutive periods. If you select Monthly granularity and the current period is May, the previous period is April.
- **YoY** — year over year. Relative difference between the current period and the same period the prior year.
- **PoP vs YoY** — depending on the time period selected, the dashboard displays PoP (e.g. MoM if the selected period is Month) or YoY, choosing the most relevant comparison automatically.

**Seller metrics**

- **Ordered Product Sales** — revenue from orders placed by customers, reported when customers place orders (even if not yet shipped).
- **ASP** — Average Selling Price. Revenue divided by Units Ordered.
- **AOV** — Average Order Value. Revenue divided by Order Count.

**Vendor metrics**

- **Shipped COGS** — revenue recognized by Amazon's accounting, based on items physically shipped from Amazon's fulfillment centers to customers.
- **Shipped Revenue** — Shipped COGS multiplied by (1 + Retail Margin %). Amazon's retail price for the units it shipped.
- **Ordered Revenue** — revenue based on customer orders, analogous to Seller Central's Ordered Product Sales. Represents consumer demand at retail prices.
- **Ordered Units** — units that customers have ordered, regardless of whether Amazon has yet shipped them. Represents consumer demand volume.
- **Glance Views** — product detail page views reported in Vendor Central.
- **Blended Revenue Metrics** — when combining Seller and Vendor data, DataHawk offers two complementary aggregations: Total Earned Revenue (Seller Ordered Product Sales + Vendor Shipped COGS) and Total Ordered Revenue (Seller Ordered Product Sales + Vendor Ordered Revenue). See the Core Metric Definitions table below.

**Advertising metrics**

- **ACoS** — Advertising Cost of Sale. Ad Spend divided by Ad Sales.
- **RoAS** — Return on Ad Spend. Ad Sales divided by Ad Spend.
- **TACoS** — Total Advertising Cost of Sale. Ad Spend divided by Total Revenue. Available in two versions — see the Core Metric Definitions table below.
- **SP** — Sponsored Products ads.
- **SB** — Sponsored Brands ads.
- **SD** — Sponsored Display ads.

### Core metric definitions

| Metric | Seller component | Vendor component | Formula | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Total Earned Revenue** | Ordered Product Sales | Shipped COGS | Seller Sales + Vendor Shipped COGS | Brand's actual earned revenue from Amazon |
| **Total Ordered Revenue** | Ordered Product Sales | Ordered Revenue | Seller Sales + Vendor Ordered Revenue | Consumer demand at retail level; benchmarking |
| **TACoS (Ordered Revenue basis)** | | | Ad Spend / Total Ordered Revenue | Advertising efficiency vs. retail demand, benchmark-comparable |
| **TACoS (Earned Revenue basis)** | | | Ad Spend / Total Earned Revenue | Ad spend vs. earned revenue, supplementary and not benchmark-comparable |
| **Total Units Ordered** | Units Ordered | Units Ordered | Seller Units + Vendor Units Ordered | Consumer demand volume |
| **Total Page Views** | Page Views | Glance Views | Seller Page Views + Vendor Glance Views | Product page reach |
| **Unit View Percentage** | | | Units Sold / Total Page Views | Conversion proxy |
| **Blended ASP (Ordered Revenue basis)** | Ordered Product Sales | Ordered Revenue | (Seller Sales + Vendor Ordered Revenue) / Total Units Ordered | Benchmarkable retail ASP |
| **Blended ASP (Earned Revenue basis)** | Ordered Product Sales | Shipped COGS | (Seller Sales + Vendor Shipped COGS) / Total Units | Average price per unit the brand actually receives |
| **Ad Metrics** | Unified | Unified | | Filterable by account type (1P / 3P) |
