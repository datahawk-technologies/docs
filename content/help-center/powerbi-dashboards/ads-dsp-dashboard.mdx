The Ads DSP Dashboard gives Amazon DSP advertisers detailed visibility into their programmatic campaigns. It covers investment efficiency, audience engagement, and conversion performance — from advertiser and order level all the way down to individual creatives.

## Questions this dashboard helps answer

- How much am I spending on DSP, and what revenue is it generating?
- What is my Return on Ad Spend (<Term>RoAS</Term>) for my DSP campaigns?
- How often are my ads being seen (impressions), and how many people click through?
- How many shoppers visit my product detail pages after seeing a DSP ad?
- What share of my DSP-attributed sales are coming from new-to-brand customers?
- Which orders and line items are performing best by spend and conversions?
- How is my budget distributed across different line item types?

## Before you start

**What you need:**

- [Amazon DSP account connected](/help-center/data-setup/ads-account) in DataHawk
- **Setup:** Download and configure the Power BI template. See [Connect to Power BI](/help-center/connect-tools/powerbi).

**Data history:** The last 60 days of load when you connect. Over time, the dashboard can show up to 1 year of data once you've been connected for at least 9 months.

| Tab | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Ads DSP Reporting** | Full DSP performance: KPIs, charts, budget distribution, and explorer |
| **Metrics Definition** | In-dashboard glossary of all DSP metrics |

## How the dashboard is structured

The main report page (Ads DSP Reporting) is organised into several analytical blocks:

**Summary table (top row)** — Key metrics for the selected period: Order Budget, Total Cost, Total Sales, Total New to Brand Sales, Impressions, Clicks, eCPM, CTR, eCP <Term>DPV</Term>, DPVR, and Purchase Rate.

**Bar and line chart** — Time-based view of Total Sales, Total Cost, and <Term>RoAS</Term>. Adjust the time granularity (day, week, month, etc.) using the calendar control at the top left.

**Budget distribution** — A 100% stacked bar chart showing how budget is allocated across different line item types.

**Metric and legend charts** — Two sets of stacked bar charts and a pie chart, each controlled by independent Metric and Legend slicers. The pie chart breaks down your chosen metric by country.

**New-to-brand gauge** — Shows the percentage of New-to-Brand Product Sales relative to Total Sales.

**Category & targeting table** — Breaks down key metrics by Category and Targeting Type (requires custom filter parameters to be configured — see below).

**Explorer** — Custom chart and table for flexible ad hoc analysis, grouped by Advertiser, Order Name, Line Item Type, Line Item Name, Creative Type, or Creative Name.

## Filters

**Date filters** — Select the date range and time granularity (Year / Quarter / Month / Week / Day).

**General filters:**

| Filter | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Advertiser** | Filter to a specific advertiser account |
| **Country** | Filter by country |
| **Currency** | Choose the display currency |

**Entity-specific filters:**

| Level | Filters available |
|---|---|
| **Order** | Order Name |
| **Line Item** | Line Item Name, Line Item Type |
| **Creative** | Creative Type, Creative Name, Creative Size |

**Custom filters** — Category, Targeting Type, Audience, and other dimensions derived from your Order or Line Item names. Requires configuration in the Data Model (see below).

## Setting up custom filters

Custom filters work by extracting keywords from your Order or Line Item names. To configure them:

- In Power BI, click the three dots next to the **Data Model** and go to **Settings \> Parameters**.
- For each parameter (Category, Targeting Type, Audience, etc.), enter the terms that appear in your Order or Line Item names, separated by commas.
- **Parameters are case-sensitive** and must match exactly. `Toys` and `TOYS` are different values.
- Leave a parameter blank if you don't use it.

## For analysts & developers

### Metrics reference

- **Impressions** — Number of times your ads were displayed
- **Clicks** — Times users clicked on your ads
- **Gross Clicks** — All clicks including potentially fraudulent or invalid ones
- **CTR** — Clicks ÷ Impressions
- **Detail Page Views (<Term>DPV</Term>)** — Product detail page views within 14 days of an ad click or view
- **Detail Page Views Rate (DPVR)** — DPV ÷ Impressions
- **Total Cost** — Total investment in DSP campaigns
- **eCPM** — Total Cost ÷ Impressions × 1,000 (cost per thousand impressions)
- **eCP DPV** — Total Cost ÷ DPV (effective cost per detail page view)
- **Order Budget** — Total budget allocated to a DSP order (latest value)
- **Line Item Budget** — Total budget allocated to a line item (latest value)
- **Sales** — Sales within 14 days of an ad click or view (promoted products only)
- **Total Sales** — Revenue generated as a direct result of DSP campaigns
- **Units Sold** — Ad-attributed units sold within 14 days of a click or view
- **Total Units Sold** — Units purchased across all brand products, attributed to the campaign
- **New to Brand Product Sales** — Sales to first-time brand shoppers from promoted products
- **Total New to Brand Sales** — Sales to first-time brand shoppers across all brand products
- **Purchases Count** — Attributed conversion events within 14 days of a click or view
- **Purchase Rate** — Purchases ÷ Impressions
- **RoAS** — Total Sales ÷ Total Cost

### Entity definitions

- **Order** — High-level budget grouping within a DSP campaign
- **Line Item** — Defines targeting, bidding, and delivery settings within an order
- **Creative** — The individual ad asset used in a campaign
- **Creative Type** — Format of the creative (display, video, etc.)
- **Creative Size** — Dimensions of the creative asset (e.g. 300×250)
