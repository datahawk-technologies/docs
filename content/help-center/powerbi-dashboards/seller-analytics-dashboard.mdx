DataHawk's Seller Analytics Dashboard gives Seller Central managers a complete view of their business across advertising, traffic, sales, profitability, and inventory — all in one place. It covers both financial performance and operational health, making it suited for daily reporting as well as deeper weekly or monthly analysis.

## Questions this dashboard helps answer

- What is my actual profit after Amazon fees, advertising spend, and returns?
- Which products are my top revenue and margin contributors this month?
- How is my traffic trending, and how well am I converting page views into orders?
- What is my current <Term>FBA</Term> inventory status, and do I need to replenish any ASINs?
- How does my advertising spend compare to my total sales (<Term>TACoS</Term>)?
- Which campaigns or products are driving the most ad-attributed revenue?
- How has my performance changed versus last week, last month, or the same period last year?

## Before you start

**What you need:**

- [Seller Central account](/help-center/data-setup/seller-account) connected in DataHawk
- [Advertising account](/help-center/data-setup/ads-account) connected in DataHawk
- <Term>COGS</Term> uploaded to DataHawk (required for P&L tabs — see [Upload Product Expenses](/help-center/data-setup/cogs-amz-upload))

**Set up:** Download and configure the Power BI template. See [Connect to Power BI](/help-center/connect-tools/powerbi).

**Data history:** Up to 2 years of historical data. The dashboard refreshes daily. The Inventory Timeline tab is the exception; it only covers the last 30 days.

| **Executive Report** | Top KPIs across sales, advertising, and inventory — weekly or monthly |
| **Product Units Economics Report** | <Term>SKU</Term>-level unit economics including revenue, fees, and margins |
| **Traffic Report** | Page views, sessions, conversion rate, and <Term>Buy Box</Term> win rate |
| **Sales Report** | Orders, units sold, revenue, and average selling/order values |
| **P&L Report** | Full profit and loss: gross revenue, fees, <Term>COGS</Term>, marketing, and net profit |
| **Profit Explorer** | Flexible P&L exploration by product, brand, tag, or <Term>marketplace</Term> |
| **Advertising — Advertising** | Advertising performance overview of the account |
| **Advertising — Campaign** | Campaign-level ad performance (spend, sales, <Term>ACoS</Term>, <Term>RoAS</Term>, <Term>TACoS</Term>) |
| **Advertising — Product** | Product-level ad performance for Sponsored Products and Display |
| **<Term>FBA</Term> Recommendations** | Current FBA stock levels with Amazon and custom restock recommendations |
| **Inventory Timeline** | 30-day history of inventory movements and fulfillment center status |
| **Explorer** | Custom cross-metric analysis with flexible dimensions |
| **Download Center** | Export any combination of metrics to a flat file |
| **Product Catalog** | Reference table of all tracked products and their attributes |

## Time periods and comparisons

Most tabs let you switch between **Weekly**, **Monthly**, and **Quarterly** views. The look-back window is **2 years** (except Inventory Timeline, which covers the last 30 days).

**Week definition:** A week starts on Sunday.

**What's selected by default:** When you open the dashboard, it preselects the most recently completed week, the current month, or the current quarter — depending on the view.

### Comparison types

- **PoP (Period over Period)** — % change versus the previous comparable period (WoW / MoM / QoQ, depending on granularity)
- **YoY (Year over Year)** — % change versus the same period, prior year
- **YTD (Year to Date)** — cumulative from January 1 to the end of the chosen reporting period
- **Last 12 Months** — rolling 12-month total

<Callout type="info">
  For weekly granularity, YTD usually gives more useful context than Last 12 Months.
</Callout>

### How changes display

- **Simple metrics** (e.g. revenue, units) show changes as a **percentage**.
- **Ratio metrics** (e.g. conversion rate, <Term>ACoS</Term>) show changes in **bps** (basis points — 100 bps = 1 percentage point).

## Dashboard filters

All tabs share a common filter panel at the top. The same data can be sliced by:

| Filter | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Seller** | Filter to one or more seller accounts |
| **Marketplace** | Filter to specific Amazon marketplaces |
| **Brand** | Filter by brand name |
| **<Term>ASIN</Term>** | Filter to specific products |
| **Currency** | Choose the display currency for monetary metrics |
| **Tag** | Filter to products assigned a specific tag in DataHawk |

<Callout type="warn">
  The **tag filter** only shows tags for products that appeared in any dashboard report within the last 2 years.
</Callout>

## For analysts & developers

### Key metric definitions

**Traffic**

- **Page Views** — Total views of product detail pages (including repeat views in a session)
- **Sessions** — Unique visits within a 24-hour window (multiple pages = one session)
- **Conversion (Page View to Order)** — Order count ÷ Page Views
- **Buy Box Win %** — % of page views where your listing was the featured offer

**Sales**

- **Sales (Orders)** — Revenue from actual orders, including <Term>VAT</Term> (except US marketplace) and excluding shipping price
- **Order Count** — Total individual customer orders
- **Units Sold** — Total units sold
- **ASP** — Average Selling Price (Sales ÷ Units Sold)
- **AOV** — Average Order Value (Sales ÷ Order Count)

**P&L**

- **Gross Revenue** — Sales including <Term>VAT</Term>/tax charges
- **Refunds** — Returns, guarantees, chargebacks, and related fees
- **Refund Rate** — Refunds as a % of Gross Revenue
- **Net Revenue** — Gross Revenue minus Refunds
- **COGS** — User-uploaded cost of goods sold applied to items sold and returned
- **Gross Profit** — Net Revenue minus COGS
- **Gross Margin** — Gross Profit ÷ Net Revenue
- **Amazon Fees** — FBA/<Term>FBM</Term> fulfillment fees, referral fees, account fees, storage, and service fees
- **Marketing** — Advertising costs, coupons, and promotions
- **Operating Profit** — Gross Profit minus Amazon Fees minus Marketing
- **Adjusted Operating Profit** — Operating Profit plus Adjustments
- **Tax Expenses** — Taxes owed on the adjusted operating profit
- **Tax Adjustments** — Manual adjustments to the tax line
- **Net Taxes** — Tax Expenses plus Tax Adjustments
- **Net Profit** — Adjusted Operating Profit minus Net Taxes

**Advertising**

- **Ad Spend** — Total advertising spend
- **Ad Sales** — Revenue attributed to advertising (split into Same-SKU and Other-SKU)
- **Ad Sales Ratio** — Ad Sales as % of Net Revenue
- **ACoS** — Ad Spend ÷ Ad Sales × 100%. Lower = more efficient
- **RoAS** — Ad Sales ÷ Ad Spend. Higher = more efficient
- **TACoS** — Ad Spend ÷ Shipped COGS × 100%. Measures advertising cost relative to total sales (not just ad-attributed sales)
- **CVR** — Orders from Ads ÷ Clicks
- **CTR** — Clicks ÷ Impressions

**Inventory (Amazon FBA)**

- **Available** — Units ready to be picked, packed, and shipped
- **Inbound Units** — Units in a shipment notified to Amazon but not yet received
- **Inbound Receiving Units** — Units being received at an Amazon fulfillment center
- **Shipped Units** — Units in transit from you to Amazon
- **Working Units** — Units being prepared for inbound shipment
- **FC Processing Units** — Units temporarily sidelined at a fulfillment center
- **FC Transfer Units** — Units being moved between fulfillment centers
- **Researching Units** — Units Amazon is investigating (usually missing or damaged)
- **Reserved for Order** — Units reserved for pending customer orders
- **Unfulfillable Units** — Units at Amazon that cannot be sold
- **<Term>MFN</Term> Fulfillable Qty** — Merchant-fulfilled units available for sale
- **Total Units** — Sum of all inventory states
- **Days of Supply** — Estimated days current inventory will last based on projected demand
- **Total Days of Supply** — Days of Supply using all inventory states, not just available
- **Total Weeks of Supply** — Amazon's weeks-of-supply estimate
- **Recommended Replenishment Quantity** — Amazon's suggested restock quantity
- **Recommended Ship Date** — Amazon's suggested ship date for the next restock
- **Alert** — Flag raised when inventory is at risk (e.g. stockout, overstock)

**Custom Inventory**

- **Total Inventory (Custom)** — Inventory total based on the components you selected in the slicer
- **Total Weeks on Hand** — Weeks of cover using your custom inventory total and sales rate
- **OOS Forecast** — Projected out-of-stock date based on sales velocity and current stock
- **Replenishment Date** — OOS Forecast minus your configured lead time
- **Replenishment Quantity** — Based on 30-day sales rate and your target weeks on hand
