The Market Intelligence dashboard is your go-to tool for understanding category-level performance on Amazon. It shows you how the top 100 best-selling products in your tracked categories are performing — including pricing, ratings, estimated sales, market share, and brand presence — giving you the competitive context to make informed decisions.

<Callout type="warn">
  Only available for Amazon
</Callout>

## Questions this dashboard helps answer

- Which brands have the largest share of sales in my category?
- How has estimated market share evolved over the past 6 or 12 months?
- What is the typical price range for best-selling products in my category?
- Which products consistently appear in the Top 100, and which come and go?
- How does my brand's Best Sellers Rank (<Term>BSR</Term>) compare to competitors?
- Are there new brands or products gaining traction in my category?
- What is the estimated total market size (in sales) for a given category?

## Before you start

**What you need:**

- At least one Amazon category added as a source in DataHawk (go to **Sources** in the DataHawk app)

**Data coverage:** The dashboard analyses the top 100 best-selling products in each tracked category.

**Setup:** Download and configure the Power BI template. See [Connect to Power BI](/help-center/connect-tools/powerbi).

## Dashboard tabs at a glance

| Tab | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Market Charts** | Summary metrics, bar charts, line charts, and a scatter chart for competitive comparison |
| **Market Overview** | Totals for all entities (<Term>marketplace</Term>, category, brand, product) over the selected timeframe |
| **Market History** | Monthly trends for your selected entities and metrics |

## General controls

All three tabs share the same set of filters:

| Filter | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Timeframe** | Set the date range for analysis |
| **Marketplace** | Filter to Amazon or Walmart, and specific country |
| **Category** | Filter to one or more tracked categories |
| **Brand** | Filter to one or more brands |
| **Product** | Filter to specific products |

Each tab also includes a shortcut to **Add Sources**, letting you add more categories or products directly from within the dashboard.

## Tab by tab

**Market Charts** — A visual exploration tool for comparing market data. Includes:

- A **summary bar** with aggregate totals for the selected filters
- A **bar chart** comparing a chosen primary metric across a chosen dimension (e.g. Estimated Market Share by Brand)
- A **line chart** showing the evolution of that metric over time for a selected entity (click any bar to focus on it)
- A **scatter chart** adding a second metric dimension for richer comparison (e.g. Market Share vs. Median Price by brand)

This tab is best for answering "who is winning?" and "how are rankings changing?"

**Market Overview** — A table showing aggregated totals for your selected entities over the selected timeframe. Click on any row to see metrics specific to that entity. Use this tab for snapshot comparisons — e.g. all brands in a category ranked by estimated market share.

**Market History** — A monthly breakdown of how your selected entities have performed over time. Includes a line chart comparing the primary metric across brands, and a detailed table below it. Click on a row to focus on a specific entity. Use this tab for trend analysis — e.g. how a brand's <Term>BSR</Term> has moved over 12 months.

## A note on unknown or missing data

In some cases, DataHawk cannot collect complete listing details for a product in a given period. When this happens, sales are still **estimated using virtual rank signals**, so overall market totals and trends remain accurate. However, the specific product or brand behind those sales cannot be reliably identified, and will appear as *XXXXXXXXXX* or *Unknown*.

These estimates reflect real market activity but lack item-level metadata. This is expected behaviour and does not indicate a data error.

## For analysts & developers

**Category entities** — the subjects around which market data is organised

- **Marketplace** — Amazon or Walmart, by country
- **Category** — A product group as defined by Amazon or Walmart
- **Brand** — A brand name associated with products in the category
- **Product** — An individual listing. Products in the Top 100 selling products in a category are called Category Best Sellers

**Product metrics**

- **Estimated Sales** — Estimated sales for a product at the given date. Sum aggregations provided
- **Estimated Sales/Product/Month** — Estimated monthly sales per product. Min, max, and median aggregations provided

**Market KPIs**

- **BSR (Best Seller Rank)** — Numerical ranking based on sales performance within a category. Lower = more sales
- **Presence in the Top 100** — The share of time a product appeared in the Top 100 for a given category. Higher = more consistent best seller status
- **Estimated Market Share** — Approximate % of total category sales attributed to a brand or product. Higher = larger share of the category
