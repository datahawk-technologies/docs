AI Copywriter's output depends as much on what you feed it as on the model behind it — choosing the right listing, refreshing a few times, and editing before publishing all matter. The sections below cover the habits we see give the biggest lift in conversion and rank.

## Pick the right product

AI Copywriter works best when:

- The product page has accurate info (correct attributes, brand, category)
- The product has at least some sales or search-volume history (so we have keyword data)
- The listing isn't already running an active A/B test that you'd disrupt

## Refresh several times before deciding

AI output varies. The first suggestion is rarely the best. Click **Refresh** two or three times and pick the version that reads best to a human shopper.

## Compare side by side

Use the side-by-side view. If the optimized version doesn't clearly win on:

- Keyword coverage (right-hand panel)
- Readability for a real shopper
- Compliance with Amazon's rules

…then either refresh again or stick with your original.

## Edit before publishing

Don't paste raw AI output into Seller Central. Read it once, fix anything stilted, and only then publish.

## A/B test broad rollouts

If you're considering rewriting many listings:

- Start with one <Term>ASIN</Term>
- Wait at least 7 days for performance to stabilise
- Compare conversion and rank before/after
- Roll out to similar ASINs only if results are positive

## Keep your originals

Save existing copy somewhere — a sheet, a doc, anywhere — before you publish a change. If performance drops, you'll want to revert quickly.

## When NOT to use AI Copywriter

- **Brand-protected listings** that need exact terms (legal claims, certifications, trademarks)
- **New product launches** where you don't yet know what keywords matter — start with Keyword Research first
- **Compliance-heavy categories** (medical, supplements) where every claim needs review by your team
