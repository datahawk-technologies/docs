DataHawk includes five modules that sit on top of your data and help you act on it. They serve different purposes — this page helps you understand what each one does and when to use it.

## At a glance

| Module | What it does | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Alerts** | Notifies you when something changes on your tracked products | Monitoring & reacting quickly |
| **Insights**| Surfaces patterns and anomalies in your data automatically | Spotting trends without manual analysis |
| **AI Copywriter** | Generates and optimizes Amazon listing content using AI | Writing and improving product listings |
| 🆕 **Sherlock** | Diagnoses *why* your performance changed and guides you to root causes | Understanding what happened and what to do |
| 🆕 **<Term>MCP</Term>** | Connects DataHawk to your favorite AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT, n8n, and more) | Asking questions and automating workflows in the tools you already use |

## Alerts — Stay on top of changes

Alerts monitors your tracked products around the clock and sends you a notification when a condition you care about is met.

You define the rules: for example, "notify me when the <Term>Buy Box</Term> price of <Term>ASIN</Term> X drops below £15" or "alert me when my Best Seller Rank drops by more than 20%". DataHawk checks the conditions daily and delivers alerts to your inbox or dashboard.

**Use Alerts when you want to:**

- Know immediately when a competitor changes their price
- Catch a <Term>Buy Box</Term> loss before it impacts your sales
- Monitor review count or rating changes on key ASINs
- Get notified when inventory drops below a threshold

Alerts is reactive — it tells you *that* something changed. To understand *why* it changed, use Sherlock.

[Go to Alerts →](/help-center/modules/alerts)

## Insights — Automated pattern detection

Insights analyzes your data automatically and surfaces observations you might not find on your own — without you having to build a query or set a rule in advance.

It looks across your product catalog, sales trends, keyword positions, and advertising performance to highlight what's moving, what's stagnant, and where there may be an opportunity or a risk.

**Use Insights when you want to:**

- Get a regular digest of what's happening across your account
- Discover underperforming products without hunting for them manually
- Identify keyword ranking shifts across your catalog
- Spot advertising inefficiencies (e.g., high spend, low return)

Insights is proactive — it brings things to your attention. You don't need to know what to look for in advance.

[Go to Insights →](/help-center/modules/insights)

## AI Copywriter — Write better listings

The AI Copywriter helps you create and optimize Amazon product listings — titles, bullet points, and descriptions — using AI. It takes your product information and generates listing content that is structured for readability and keyword relevance.

**Use AI Copywriter when you want to:**

- Write a listing for a new product from scratch
- Improve an existing listing's title or bullet points
- Generate listing variations for A/B testing
- Scale listing creation across a large catalog

[Go to AI Copywriter →](/help-center/modules/ai-copywriter)

## Sherlock — Diagnose performance changes

Sherlock is DataHawk's AI-powered diagnostic engine. When your sales, rankings, or revenue change, Sherlock helps you understand *why* — breaking down the contributing factors and guiding you to the root cause.

You can interact with Sherlock through its standard interface or by talking to the Sherlock Agent directly in natural language. You can also log business events (promotions, price changes, listing edits) so Sherlock can factor them into its analysis.

**Use Sherlock when you want to:**

- Investigate a sudden drop or spike in sales
- Understand what's driving a change in your <Term>BSR</Term> or conversion rate
- Get an AI-assisted explanation of your account's recent performance
- Ask questions about your data in plain language

Sherlock is diagnostic — it tells you *why* something happened and what to investigate next.

[Go to Sherlock →](/help-center/modules/sherlock)

## MCP — Bring DataHawk into your favorite AI

DataHawk's MCP server lets external AI assistants — Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, n8n, Make, and more — query your DataHawk data directly. Connect once via <Term>OAuth</Term>, then ask questions in plain English, run scheduled reports, or wire DataHawk into your own automations.

**Use MCP when you want to:**

- Ask questions about your performance inside Claude or ChatGPT
- Build scheduled Slack or email digests using n8n or Make
- Combine DataHawk numbers with tools you already use (Gmail, Google Sheets, Slack)
- Build custom apps that read your DataHawk data

[MCP Setup →](/help-center/modules/datahawkmcp/setup)

## How the modules work together

<Callout type="info">
You don't need to use all five — start with whichever fits the problem you're trying to solve today.
</Callout>

These five modules complement each other. A typical workflow might look like:

1. **Insights** surfaces a drop in conversion rate on one of your ASINs
2. **Alerts** confirms the Buy Box was lost two days ago
3. **AI Copywriter** helps you refresh the listing to improve relevance while you address pricing
4. **Sherlock** diagnoses the root cause — a competitor undercut your price by 8%
5. **MCP** lets you ask Claude to draft a Slack update for your team, with the latest numbers pulled live

### Scenario 1: Conversion rate drops on a key ASIN

A common Monday-morning problem.

1. **Insights** flags a conversion rate drop on ASIN B0XXXXXX in your Findings or Anomalies list
2. **Alerts** confirms the Buy Box was lost two days ago — the alert email arrived Sunday morning
3. **Sherlock** diagnoses the root cause: a competitor undercut your price by 8%
4. **MCP** lets you ask Claude to draft a Slack update for your team, with the latest numbers pulled live

**Time saved:** what used to be a half-day of manual digging now takes about 20 minutes.

### Scenario 2: Catching an inventory risk before it costs sales

1. **Alerts** notifies you when a tracked ASIN drops below 30 days of cover
2. **Insights** flags any anomaly in units-sold velocity — useful when demand is rising faster than your last forecast assumed
3. **Sherlock** answers "is my stock health good across the catalog?" in plain English, prioritized by impact
4. **MCP** wires this into n8n or Make to trigger a daily Slack digest in your inventory channel

### Scenario 3: Weekly performance review

A weekly habit that ties everything together.

1. **MCP** pulls a fresh executive summary into Claude or ChatGPT every Monday morning — revenue, <Term>ACOS</Term>, top movers, biggest drops
2. **Insights** has the week's Findings ready to review (top-performing ad campaigns, listings that need refreshing)
3. **Sherlock** is on hand for any "why did this happen?" follow-ups
4. **AI Copywriter** helps you action listing changes before the week starts
