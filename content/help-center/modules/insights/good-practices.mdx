Good Practices sit alongside Findings and Anomalies in the Insights feed, but they work differently — they don't fire from your data. Think of them as an always-available reference library for fundamentals like <Term>FBA</Term>, Sponsored Products, <Term>Buy Box</Term>, and listing imagery. Useful for newcomers and for sanity-checking a team's setup.

## What Good Practices cover

Examples of topics:

- How to make the most of Amazon <Term>FBA</Term>
- How to use Amazon Sponsored Products effectively
- How to increase conversion with great listing images
- How to maximize reach with Amazon Multi-Channel Fulfillment
- How to win the <Term>Buy Box</Term>
- How to choose and optimize backend search terms
- And more

## When to use them

- **You're new to selling on Amazon or Walmart** — Good Practices give you a quick reference for the fundamentals.
- **You want a sanity check** on whether you're following the basics.
- **You're onboarding a teammate** — these are good reading for someone joining your eCommerce operation.
