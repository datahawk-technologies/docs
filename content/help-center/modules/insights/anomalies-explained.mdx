Anomalies are the day-to-day half of Insights — DataHawk compares each tracked metric against a 7-day baseline and flags anything outside the expected confidence range. This page covers how to read an anomaly chart and the full list of metrics monitored across Finance, Ads, SEO, Product, and Market.

## How to read an anomaly chart

Each anomaly comes with a chart that shows the metric over time, the expected range, and where the anomaly was detected.

**Anomaly indicator (top-right box):**

- **Observed** — the actual value on a specific date
- **Expected** — the value predicted by a 7-day moving average
- **Anomaly percentage** — how far the observed value deviates from expected. Outside the confidence range = flagged as an anomaly.

**Chart components:**

- **Observed value (solid line)** — actual recorded value over time. Blue for normal days; yellow when an anomaly was detected.
- **Expected value (dashed line)** — 7-day moving average, excluding anomalies. The smoothed trend line.
- **Confidence range (shaded area)** — three standard deviations around the expected value. The natural range of fluctuation.

## Finance anomalies

Generated for products you sell or manage — auto-selected from the orders account connected to your <Term>workspace</Term>. Grouped by Seller Central account.

We analyze 15 days of historical order data at the <Term>marketplace</Term> and product level to identify unexpected events. Because Amazon delivers data with a partial delay, **anomalies appear two days after the event** (e.g. Wednesday's email flags Monday's anomaly). The delay ensures we work with complete data.

Includes:

- **Gross Sales Anomaly** — unexpected change in gross sales
- **Units Sold Anomaly** — unexpected change in units sold
- **Average Selling Price Anomaly** — outlying price value
- **Taxes Anomaly** — outlying tax value
- **Shipping Fee Anomaly** — outlying shipping cost
- And other fee anomalies listed above

## SEO anomalies

Generated for products you sell **and** track. Use the **Set as owned** feature in Sources to help us identify which products are yours.

- **Search Ranking Anomaly** — unexpected change in organic rank for a keyword. If historical data is missing for a day, we leave the gap unfilled.
- **Reach Score Anomaly** — unexpected change in reach score (estimated number of times a product has been seen across tracked keywords). Built on our Keyword Search Volume data.

## Product anomalies

Generated for products you sell **and** track.

- **Rating Score Anomaly** — unexpected change in a product's rating score

## Market anomalies

Generated for the categories your tracked products rank in.

- **Median Number of Ratings — Top 100 per Category** — unusual change in the median across the top 100 in a category
- **Median Rating Score — Top 100 per Category** — same, for rating score
- **Median Price — Top 100 per Category** — same, for price
- **Sales Rank Anomalies** — unexpected change in a product's sales rank within a category
