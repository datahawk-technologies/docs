Findings are the weekly half of Insights — curated recommendations rather than daily anomaly alerts. Each Finding combines your tracked-product data with category benchmarks to surface where SEO improvements, competitor watching, or ad pruning will pay off most. This page walks through each of the four types in turn.

## The four Finding types

| Finding | Practice | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Improve your listings organically | SEO | Weekly |
| Competitive products weekly digest | Market | Weekly |
| Review ineffective Sponsored Product Ads campaigns | Ads | Weekly |
| Review top-performing Sponsored Product Ads campaigns | Ads | Weekly |

## Improve your listings organically

A weekly list of products where SEO improvements are most likely to pay off. Supported on all Amazon marketplaces.

We pick products that meet at least one of these conditions:

1. **Could sell more by ranking higher** — by comparing your products' competitors and their estimated monthly sales, we surface the 10 with the most potential against the competition
2. **Could rank for more keywords** — by comparing rank across your products and competitors, we surface those that rank lowest or least often
3. **Are top performers** worth keeping optimized — products with the highest estimated sales (in your local currency)

<Callout type="info">
  To enhance the listings flagged here, use the Keyword Research datasets to find high-potential terms.
</Callout>

## Competitive products weekly digest

A weekly list of products competing with yours, so you can evaluate threats and opportunities. Supported on all Amazon marketplaces.

We identify competitors using an in-house competitiveness score that looks at:

1. Your **product pages** — competitors appearing in sponsored or organic placements (e.g. "related to" sections)
2. **Keywords** — competitors ranking organically or via ads on the same keywords as you
3. **Categories** — products in the same category as yours

We use parent ASINs only when computing competitors.

**"New" vs. "Emerging" competitors:**

- **New** — competitors with under 100 reviews, observed for the first time the previous week
- **Emerging** — competitors with over 100 reviews that just surfaced in our score list

We use review counts because product pages and ASINs don't have a public upload date — review count is a better signal of how long a product has been on the digital shelf.

## Review ineffective Sponsored Product Ads campaigns

A weekly list of ad campaigns that could be improved by reducing or stopping spend on certain targets (ASINs and keywords). Supported on all Amazon marketplaces.

We compute a custom efficiency score for each target using its <Term>RoAS</Term> and CTR, then rank campaigns by global <Term>RoAS</Term>. Targets with poor scores are flagged for spend reduction.

<Callout type="info">
  Run the *Campaign Optimisation Recommendation – Sponsored Products* report for actionable advice on each target.
</Callout>

## Review top-performing Sponsored Product Ads campaigns

The mirror image — a weekly list of campaigns where increasing spend on specific targets is likely to pay off. Supported on all Amazon marketplaces.

Same scoring logic; targets with strong scores are flagged for more aggressive spending.

## How to receive Findings

- **"Improve listings organically"** and **"Competitive products weekly digest"** — generated for products you own and have tracked. Tag your products as **Owned** in Sources for accurate results.
- **"Review ineffective" and "Review top-performing" Sponsored Product Ads** — generated for ad campaigns in the ad accounts connected to your <Term>workspace</Term>.
