Finished prompt templates you fill in and run; for recurring reports, weekly reviews, and one-click deep-dives. Each one produces a polished output you can drop into Slack, email, or a team meeting.

<Callout type="info">
  **Use these when** you want a polished, repeatable report. **Write your own** (see [How to write effective MCP prompts](/help-center/modules/datahawkmcp/write-prompts)) when you have a one-off question that doesn't fit any template here.
</Callout>

## How to use a ready-to-use prompt

Each template has three things you'll work with: **Variables to set**, **What to expect**, and **The prompt** itself.

<Steps>

<Step>
### Open the prompt

Pick the template that matches what you want to know.
</Step>

<Step>
### Read the "Variables to set" section

These are the `[FIELD_NAME]` placeholders you'll replace (<Term>marketplace</Term>, thresholds, <Term>ASIN</Term> list, etc.).
</Step>

<Step>
### Copy the prompt text

Copy the prompt from the code block.
</Step>

<Step>
### Replace each placeholder

Swap every `[FIELD_NAME]` with your own values.
</Step>

<Step>
### Paste into your AI client and run

Paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or another MCP-connected client and run.
</Step>

</Steps>

The "What to expect" section on each page tells you what the output will look like, so you'll know whether a run came back the way it should.

## Tune the thresholds for your scale

Most templates include threshold values you'll want to adjust to your business.

A \$500 spend threshold feels like signal at \$50K/week revenue and noise at \$5M/week. Same for <Term>ACOS</Term> percentages, days of cover, and rank-drop counts; what's a flag for one business is normal for another.

When you first run a template, treat the suggested defaults as a starting point. After a week or two of running it, you'll know which thresholds to tighten or loosen.

<Accordion title='Weekly Executive Sunnary'>
A once-a-week, copy-paste prompt to generate a complete executive summary of your Amazon business. Designed for Claude and ChatGPT with DataHawk MCP connected.

**Variables to set before running**

- `[MARKETPLACE_SCOPE]`: *e.g., "the US marketplace"; "the US, UK, and DE marketplaces"; "all my active marketplaces"*
- `[ACOS_FLAG]`: ACOS threshold for flagging campaigns. *Start at 40 (%)*
- `[SPEND_FLAG]`: Spend threshold for flagging campaigns. *Tune to your scale: $500 for ~$50K/week sellers, $5,000 for ~$5M/week sellers*
- `[HIGH_SPEND_FLAG]`: Higher spend threshold for ROAS-flag campaigns. *Roughly 2× your `[SPEND_FLAG]`*

**What to expect**

Typical output structure (actual length and formatting vary based on your data and AI client):

- **Topline**: a table showing this week's metrics, last week's, and the percent change for each
- **Marketplace breakdown**: same metrics, one row per marketplace, with any marketplace down >15% flagged
- **Top movers**: two ranked lists of 5 ASINs each (growth and decline), with a one-line reason for each decliner
- **Inventory risk**: A short list of at-risk ASINs: Or "no inventory risks this week", which is a valid result
- **Advertising flags**: A list of campaigns hitting your thresholds: Often empty if nothing crosses them
- **One thing to watch**: a single bolded recommendation at the bottom

**The prompt**
```
Act as my senior analyst. Prepare my weekly Amazon executive summary for the week ending last Sunday.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE].

Use DataHawk MCP. Cite the DataHawk numbers and briefly explain anything that looks anomalous. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. TOPLINE (last 7 days vs. prior 7 days)
   - Gross revenue, units ordered, average selling price
   - Ad spend, ACOS, ROAS
   - Call out any metric that moved more than 10% week-over-week

2. MARKETPLACE BREAKDOWN
   - Same metrics split by marketplace
   - Flag any marketplace with a week-over-week revenue drop above 15%

3. TOP MOVERS
   - Top 5 ASINs by revenue growth (absolute $) last 7 days
   - Top 5 ASINs by revenue decline last 7 days
   - For each decliner, note whether the issue looks like traffic, conversion, or inventory

4. INVENTORY RISK
   - Any ASIN with fewer than 30 days of cover
   - Any ASIN currently out of stock that was in stock 7 days ago

5. ADVERTISING FLAGS
   - Campaigns with ACOS above [ACOS_FLAG]% and spend above $[SPEND_FLAG] last 7 days
   - Campaigns with ROAS below 2x and spend above $[HIGH_SPEND_FLAG] last 7 days

6. ONE THING TO WATCH NEXT WEEK
   - Your single highest-priority callout based on the data above

Format as a clean report with section headers. Use tables where helpful.
```

</Accordion>

<Accordion title='Monthly PPC Performance by Marketplace'>
A month-end PPC report split by marketplace, designed for owner or executive reporting on international advertising performance.


**Variables to set before running**

- `[REPORT_MONTH]`: Month to report, *e.g., "March 2026"*
- `[MARKETPLACE_SCOPE]`: *e.g., "all non-US marketplaces"; "all my European marketplaces"; "all my active marketplaces"*
- `[MIN_SPEND_FILTER]`: Exclude marketplaces with less than this spend. *Start at 1,000 ($)*

**What to expect**

Typical output structure (actual length and formatting vary based on your data and AI client):

- **Topline**: total ad spend, ad sales, ACOS, ROAS for the month, plus comparison to the prior month
- **Marketplace breakdown**: same metrics, one row per marketplace, ranked by spend
- **Top campaigns per marketplace**: 3 biggest spenders in each marketplace, with their ACOS and ROAS
- **Notable changes**: marketplaces where spend, ACOS, or ROAS moved more than 20% month-over-month
- **Owner summary**: a 3-sentence non-technical summary suitable for emailing leadership

**The prompt**
```
Act as my senior analyst. Prepare my weekly Amazon executive summary for the week ending last Sunday.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE].

Act as my senior PPC analyst. Prepare a monthly PPC report on my Amazon advertising for [REPORT_MONTH] using DataHawk MCP.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE].
Filter: exclude marketplaces with less than $[MIN_SPEND_FILTER] in ad spend for the month.

Cite the DataHawk numbers. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. TOPLINE
   - Total ad spend, ad sales, ACOS, ROAS for the month
   - Comparison to the prior month (absolute and percent change)

2. MARKETPLACE BREAKDOWN
   - Same metrics by marketplace, ranked by spend
   - Flag any marketplace where ACOS moved more than 20% month-over-month

3. TOP CAMPAIGNS BY MARKETPLACE
   - For each marketplace included: the top 3 campaigns by spend
   - Include each campaign's ACOS and ROAS

4. NOTABLE CHANGES
   - Any marketplace with significant performance shifts vs. prior month
   - Suggest what to investigate

5. OWNER SUMMARY
   - A 3-sentence summary suitable for emailing the owner or leadership team
   - Highlight the headline number and any action needed

Format as a clean report with tables. Tone the owner summary at the bottom for a non-technical executive reader.
```

<Accordion title='Inventory Risk Radar'>
Surface inventory problems before they become stockouts. Run weekly for best results.

**Variables to set before running**

- `[MARKETPLACE_SCOPE]`: *e.g., "the US marketplace"; "the US and UK marketplaces"*
- `[COVER_DAYS_THRESHOLD]`: Days-of-cover threshold to flag low stock. *Start at 30*
- `[UNSELLABLE_PCT_THRESHOLD]`: Unsellable percentage to flag. *Start at 10 (%)*
- `[FULFILLMENT_TYPE]`, *FBA, MFN, or both, pick what applies to your business*

**What to expect**

Typical output structure (actual length and formatting vary based on your data and AI client):

- **Currently out of stock**: A ranked table by lost revenue: Could be empty if nothing's out of stock
- **Low cover**: a table of ASINs approaching stockout with cover days and inbound quantities
- **Inbound bottlenecks**: A short list: Often empty if your supply chain is healthy
- **Unsellable**: top ASINs with trapped inventory, with dollar values
- **Vendor-specific**: Present only if a vendor account is connected: Otherwise skipped
- **Action plan**: 3 specific items to fix this week, each with the dollar value at stake

**The prompt**
```
Act as my inventory planner. Run an inventory risk scan on my Amazon business using DataHawk MCP.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE], [FULFILLMENT_TYPE] inventory.

Cite the DataHawk numbers. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. CURRENT OUT OF STOCK
   - All ASINs currently out of stock
   - Rank them by last 30 days' revenue (biggest revenue at risk first)

2. LOW COVER (approaching stockout)
   - ASINs with on-hand inventory below [COVER_DAYS_THRESHOLD] days of cover, using last 30 days' average daily units
   - Include current on-hand quantity, inbound shipped quantity, and inbound working quantity

3. INBOUND BOTTLENECKS
   - Any ASIN where inbound working quantity is more than 50% of total inbound and has been stuck for 14+ days

4. UNSELLABLE
   - ASINs where unsellable quantity is more than [UNSELLABLE_PCT_THRESHOLD]% of total on-hand
   - Flag the top 5 by dollar value trapped

5. VENDOR-SPECIFIC (skip if no vendor account is connected)
   - Any ASIN with confirmation rate below 90% or fill rate below 85%
   - Aged 90+ days sellable inventory by dollar value

6. ACTION PLAN
   - Which 3 items need immediate action
   - For each: what action, and what it's worth if resolved

Format as a clean report. Use tables where helpful.
```

</Accordion>

- **New competitors**: ASINs where new sellers appeared in the period
- **Rating / review shifts**: Any ASINs trending down: Could be empty if your reviews are stable
- **Recommended actions**: 3 specific pricing moves with predicted impact on buy box percentage


**The prompt**
```
Act as my pricing strategist. Analyze buy box and competitive pricing for my top [TOP_N_ASINS] ASINs by revenue (last 30 days) using DataHawk MCP.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE], [LOOKBACK_PERIOD] lookback.

Cite the DataHawk numbers and explain your reasoning briefly. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. BUY BOX LOSS
   - ASINs where buy box percentage dropped more than [BUY_BOX_DROP_THRESHOLD] percentage points over the lookback period
   - For each: who's winning the buy box now, their price vs. mine

2. PRICE CHANGES
   - My price changes during the lookback period: Which ASINs, up or down, by how much
   - Any ASIN where I lowered the price but my buy box percentage still dropped?

3. NEW COMPETITORS
   - ASINs where the number of competing sellers increased by 2 or more in the last 30 days

4. RATING / REVIEW SHIFTS
   - Any of my top ASINs with a rating drop greater than 0.2 points in the last 30 days
   - Any with review count growing less than 1 per day (potentially declining interest)

5. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
   - Top 3 pricing moves to consider, with expected impact on buy box percentage
   - Any product that should go into defensive mode (matching the lowest competitor)

Format as a clean report. Use tables where helpful.
```

<Accordion title='ACOS Deep-dive'>
A once-a-week, copy-paste prompt to generate a complete executive summary of your Amazon business. Designed for Claude and ChatGPT with DataHawk MCP connected.

**Variables to set before running**

- `[MARKETPLACE_SCOPE]`: *e.g., "the US marketplace"*
- `[ANALYSIS_PERIOD]`: *e.g., "last 28 days"*
- `[ACOS_DRILL_THRESHOLD]`: Campaigns above this ACOS get flagged for review. *Start at 35 (%)*
- `[TOP_N_CAMPAIGNS]`: How many campaigns to drill into. *Start at 10*

**What to expect**

Typical output structure (actual length and formatting vary based on your data and AI client):

- **Overall ACOS trend**: a small table of weekly ACOS plus a one-line attribution (driven by spend, sales, or both)
- **ACOS by ad type**: a table comparing Sponsored Products, Brands, and Display
- **Campaign-level drill**: a table of the top N campaigns with current ACOS, week-over-week change, and any flags
- **Keyword investigation**: smaller tables for the 3 worst campaigns, listing top-spending keywords
- **Recommended actions**: a bulleted list of specific campaigns and keywords to pause, reduce, or investigate

**The prompt**
```
Act as my senior PPC analyst. Do an ACOS deep-dive on my Amazon advertising using DataHawk MCP.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE], [ANALYSIS_PERIOD].

Cite the DataHawk numbers and briefly explain your reasoning. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. OVERALL ACOS TREND
   - Weekly ACOS for the analysis period, compared to the equivalent prior period
   - Was the change driven primarily by spend, sales, or both?

2. ACOS BY AD TYPE
   - Split ACOS by Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display
   - Which ad type moved the most?

3. CAMPAIGN-LEVEL DRILL
   - Top [TOP_N_CAMPAIGNS] campaigns by spend
   - For each: ACOS, week-over-week change, units attributed
   - Flag any campaign with ACOS above [ACOS_DRILL_THRESHOLD]% that grew spend week-over-week

4. KEYWORD INVESTIGATION (for the 3 worst campaigns from step 3)
   - Top spending keywords and their ACOS
   - Any keyword with CPC up more than 20% but conversion rate down?

5. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
   - What to pause, what to reduce bids on, what to investigate further
   - Be specific: Name campaigns and keywords

Format as a clean report. Use tables where helpful.
```

</Accordion>

**Variables to set before running**

- **Action plan**: top 3 keywords to recover with the lever to pull for each (content, ads, pricing, or inventory)

**The prompt**
```
Act as my SEO analyst. Do a keyword rank recovery analysis on my top [TOP_N_ASINS] ASINs by revenue using DataHawk MCP.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE], [LOOKBACK_PERIOD] lookback.

Cite the DataHawk numbers and explain your reasoning briefly. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. LOST RANKINGS
   - For each top ASIN: keywords where organic rank dropped [RANK_DROP_THRESHOLD] or more positions during the lookback period
   - Include the keyword, current rank, rank at the start of the lookback, and search volume if available

2. SPONSORED VS. ORGANIC GAP
   - Keywords where I rank in the top 10 sponsored but not in the top 30 organic
   - These are opportunities to convert paid visibility into organic rank

3. COMPETITOR GAINS
   - For the 5 keywords with the biggest drops, who's moved up to take the ranking?
   - Is there a pattern (same brand repeatedly, or scattered)?

4. POSSIBLE CAUSES
   - Cross-reference with: catalog changes on each ASIN (title, bullets, images), review velocity drop, inventory stockouts in the period, and price changes

5. ACTION PLAN
   - Top 3 keywords to prioritize recovering, and why
   - What lever to pull for each (content, ads, pricing, or inventory)

Format as a clean report. Use tables where helpful.
```

   - Total estimated category sales for the period

### Maintenance

<Accordions>
<Accordion title='Catalog & Keyword Hygiene Audit'>
Surface stale ASINs and keywords no longer worth tracking, plus new ASINs and keywords you might be missing. Run quarterly to keep tracking lean.

**Variables to set before running**

- `[MARKETPLACE_SCOPE]`: *e.g., "the US marketplace"*
- `[STALE_THRESHOLD_DAYS]`: Days of low or no activity to flag a tracked item as stale. *Start at 90*
- `[NEW_ASIN_REVENUE_FLOOR]`: Minimum revenue an untracked ASIN must have to be worth adding. *Tune to your scale: $1,000 for ~$50K/week sellers, $10,000 for ~$5M/week sellers*

**What to expect**

Typical output structure (actual length and formatting vary based on your data and AI client):

- **Stale tracked ASINs**: ASINs in your DataHawk that had low or no activity in the threshold period
- **Untracked but earning ASINs**: ASINs not tracked in DataHawk but generating real revenue
- **Stale tracked keywords**: tracked keywords with no rank movement or recorded activity
- **Missing keywords**: high-traffic keywords where your products appear but the keyword isn't tracked
- **Recommended cleanup**: top 10 to remove and top 10 to add


**The prompt**
```
Act as my catalog manager. Run a hygiene audit on my DataHawk tracking using MCP.

Scope: [MARKETPLACE_SCOPE].
Stale threshold: [STALE_THRESHOLD_DAYS] days of low or no activity.
Revenue floor for new ASINs: $[NEW_ASIN_REVENUE_FLOOR] over the last 90 days.

Cite the DataHawk numbers. If any number isn't available from DataHawk, say so rather than estimating.

1. STALE TRACKED ASINS
   - ASINs being tracked that had no orders or fewer than $100 revenue in the last [STALE_THRESHOLD_DAYS] days
   - Rank by lowest activity first

2. UNTRACKED BUT EARNING ASINS
   - ASINs generating more than $[NEW_ASIN_REVENUE_FLOOR] in the last 90 days that aren't being tracked in DataHawk
   - List with revenue contribution

3. STALE TRACKED KEYWORDS
   - Keywords being tracked that have shown no rank movement or recorded clicks in the last [STALE_THRESHOLD_DAYS] days
   - Likely candidates to remove

4. MISSING KEYWORDS
   - High-traffic keywords where my products appear in organic or sponsored results but the keyword isn't being tracked
   - Rank by traffic potential

5. RECOMMENDED CLEANUP
   - Top 10 items to remove from tracking
   - Top 10 items to add to tracking
   - Estimated impact on tracking coverage and cost

Format as a clean report. Use tables for the lists. Always show ASIN + product title side by side.
```

</Accordion>
</Accordions>

## Pin them for one-click use

Most AI clients let you save a prompt so you don't have to paste it every time:

- **Claude**: Create a Project, paste the template as your custom instructions
- **ChatGPT**: Create a custom GPT with the template baked in
- **Cursor, Lovable, Replit, n8n, Make**: Save in your prompt library or call from a workflow

Once pinned, running your weekly summary is one click.
