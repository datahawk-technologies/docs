Power BI is DataHawk's recommended dashboarding tool. DataHawk provides ready-to-use Power BI templates built on top of your <Term>Snowflake</Term> database.

<Callout type="info">
  You can be up and running in under 5 minutes.
</Callout>

## Prerequisite

- Your data sources (Amazon / Walmart accounts) should be connected and collecting data.
  - If you did not connect any data sources, go to [Set up data](/help-center/data-setup)
- An activated DataHawk-hosted **<Term>Snowflake</Term>** database.
  - If you haven't set one up yet, go to [Send data to your tool](/help-center/connect-tools)
  - Once set up, it will take up to 4 hours to show as **Enabled**
- Retrieve your credentials
  - On your DataHawk App account, **Setup \> Destinations \> Snowflake \> See Credentials**.

## Option 1 — Use a DataHawk template

DataHawk provides pre-built Power BI templates in `.pbix` format. See [Download Power BI Templates](/help-center/connect-tools/powerbi) for the full catalog and download link.

| Template | What it covers |
|---|---|
| Seller Finances & Supply Chain | <Term>SKU</Term>-level profit, orders, unit economics, replenishment |
| Product | Ratings, <Term>BSR</Term>, price, LQS, sales, estimates, <Term>buy box</Term> |
| Search | Share of voice, search ranks, keyword research |
| Advertising | Account, campaign, product, and keyword ads |
| Market Intelligence | Top-selling products, category insights, market size and share |
| Master Vendor | Revenue, <Term>COGS</Term>, inventory, ads, share of voice |
| <Term>DSP</Term> Ads | Advertiser, order, line item, and creative ads |

### Set up on Power BI Desktop

**Estimated time: under 5 minutes.**

1. Open the `.pbix` template file in Power BI Desktop.
2. In the **Home** tab, click **Transform data** to open the Power Query Editor.
3. Click **Manage Parameters**.
4. Replace the values for **Database**, **Server**, and **Warehouse** with your DataHawk Snowflake credentials.
5. Click **Close & Apply**.
6. Enter your Snowflake **username** and **password** when prompted.
7. Data will begin refreshing — this can take up to 30 minutes depending on your data volume.

<Callout type="info">
  If you run into a refresh error, try closing and re-applying the parameters once or twice, then contact the DataHawk team if the issue persists.
</Callout>

### Publish to Power BI Online (for sharing and scheduled refresh)

**Estimated time: under 5 minutes.**

<Callout type="info">
  **One-time admin step required:** Before publishing templates to Power BI Online, a Power BI administrator must enable Snowflake SSO in your tenant.

  1. Go to [app.powerbi.com/admin-portal](https://app.powerbi.com/admin-portal)
  2. Navigate to **Tenant Settings**
  3. Find **Snowflake SSO**, expand it, and enable the toggle
  4. Allow a few hours for the change to propagate across your organization
</Callout>

Once SSO is enabled:

1. Go to [app.powerbi.com](https://app.powerbi.com/) and navigate to a <Term>workspace</Term> (or create one).
2. Click **Upload** \> **Browse** and select your `.pbix` template file.
3. Hover over the newly uploaded dataset, click the **⋯** menu, and select **Settings**.
4. Under **Parameters**, replace **Database**, **Server**, and **Warehouse** with your DataHawk Snowflake credentials.
5. Under **Data source credentials**, click **Edit credentials** and enter your Snowflake **username** and **password**.
6. Under **Refresh**, enable the auto-refresh toggle and set it to run daily.
7. Data will begin refreshing — this can take up to 30 minutes.
8. Once refreshed, navigate back to your workspace to open the report.

## Option 2 — Build your own reports

If you prefer to build from scratch or connect an existing Power BI file to your DataHawk data, follow the steps below.

DataHawk's Power BI templates use **DirectQuery** mode. If you plan to build your own reports, the same mode is recommended to ensure your data stays live.

### Connect via Power BI Desktop

1. Open Power BI Desktop.
2. Click **Get Data \> More**, search for **Snowflake**, and click **Connect**.
3. Enter the **Server** and **Warehouse** values from your DataHawk Snowflake credentials page.
4. Select **Database** as the authentication mode.
5. Enter your Snowflake **username** and **password**.
6. Browse the schemas and tables, or write a custom SQL query.
7. Click **Load** (or **Transform Data** to filter before loading).

### Connect via Power BI Online

Before connecting via Power BI Online, you need to enable Snowflake SSO in your Power BI tenant — this is a one-time admin step.

<Callout type="info">
  **Admin required:** You must be an administrator of your Power BI instance to complete this.

  1. Go to [app.powerbi.com/admin-portal](https://app.powerbi.com/admin-portal).
  2. Navigate to **Tenant Settings**.
  3. Find **Snowflake SSO**, expand it, and enable the toggle.
  4. Allow a few hours for the change to propagate across your organization.
</Callout>

Once SSO is enabled, upload your `.pbix` file and point the parameters (Database, Server, Warehouse) to your DataHawk Snowflake credentials.
