Looker Studio is Google's free dashboarding tool. DataHawk connects to Looker Studio via your <Term>BigQuery</Term> database, giving you a live link between your DataHawk data and your reports.

## Prerequisites

- An activated DataHawk-hosted **BigQuery** database.
  - If you haven't set one up yet, go to [Send data to your tool](/help-center/connect-tools)
- Your data sources (Amazon / Walmart accounts) should be connected and collecting data.
  - If you did not connect any data sources, go to [Set up data](/help-center/data-setup)
- Your Google email address must be registered on your BigQuery database page
  - Go to your DataHawk App Account, **Setup \> Destinations \> BigQuery \> Access**. DataHawk uses this to share access to templates.

## Option 1 — Use a DataHawk template

DataHawk provides a ready-to-use Looker Studio template called **Analytics Essentials**, covering product, keywords, advertising, sales, finance, traffic, and inventory views — all pre-built and connected to your BigQuery data.

**To request access:**

1. In the DataHawk app, go to [Report Templates](https://analytics.datahawk.co/app/report-templates).
2. Find the Looker Studio template and click **Request Access**.
3. A DataHawk team member will share the template to your Google email.
4. You will receive an email invitation — accept it to open the report in Looker Studio.

See [Looker Studio Template](/help-center/looker-studio) for the full template guide.

## Option 2 — Connect manually to BigQuery (for custom reports)

If you want to build your own Looker Studio reports from scratch, you can connect directly to your DataHawk BigQuery database using Looker Studio's native BigQuery connector.

1. Go to [lookerstudio.google.com](https://lookerstudio.google.com/) and create a new report or data source.
2. Click **Add data** and select **BigQuery**.
3. Select the DataHawk BigQuery project associated with your workspace.
4. Choose a dataset (schema) and table, or write a custom SQL query.
5. Click **Add** to connect.

Data in Looker Studio refreshes based on BigQuery's underlying data, which DataHawk updates daily.
