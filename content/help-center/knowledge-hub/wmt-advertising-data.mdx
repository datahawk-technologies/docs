This page is the reference for Walmart Connect advertising data in DataHawk. All tables live in the `ADVERTISING` schema and are prefixed with `WMT_`. Data is refreshed daily, with a second pass 15 days after the first download since Walmart finalizes reports on a 14-day delay.

<Callout type="info">
  To connect your Walmart Connect account so these tables start populating, see [Connect a Walmart Connect Account](/help-center/data-setup/connect-account).
</Callout>

## Ad-group and item performance

These are the two foundational reports — every Walmart advertiser has them, and most analyses start here.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="WMT_AD_GROUP — Ad-group performance">

Performance metrics aggregated at the ad-group level. Use it to compare ad groups within a single campaign or roll up across campaigns.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_AD_GROUP`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="WMT_SP_AD_ITEM — Sponsored Products item performance">

Per-item performance metrics across all Sponsored Products campaigns. Use it to find your best- and worst-performing items, and to improve current or future Sponsored Products campaigns.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_SP_AD_ITEM`

<Callout type="warn">
  The legacy `WMT_SP_AD_ITEM` table previously included AD_SPEND from Sponsored Products, Brands, and Video lumped together, even though every row was labeled `SPONSORED_TYPE = 'SponsoredProducts'`. The replacement table `ADVERTISING.WMT_AD_ITEM` (released May 12, 2026) correctly attributes spend by sponsored type. See the [changelog entry](/changelog/2026-05-12-walmart-wmt-ad-item) for migration details.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Brand and category performance (1P only)

These two reports are **only available to Walmart 1P suppliers** (vendors selling directly to Walmart). 3P sellers will see these tables empty.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="WMT_BRAND — Brand performance">

Performance broken down by brand within a campaign.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_BRAND`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="WMT_CATEGORY — Category performance">

Performance broken down by the category of the items in a campaign.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_CATEGORY`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Item health

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="WMT_ITEM_HEALTH_V2 — Item Health diagnostics">

The Item Health report flags the health of sponsored items so advertisers can take corrective actions to improve performance and reach campaign goals. Use it as a pre-flight check before launching new campaigns and as a recurring sweep on existing ones.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_ITEM_HEALTH_V2`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Keyword performance

Two complementary tables describe how your items perform against the search queries customers used.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="WMT_SP_ITEM_KEYWORD — Item × keyword performance">

Indicates which keywords your advertised items are being served against and how each item performs against each keyword. Use it differently depending on campaign type:

- **Automatic campaigns:** Identify keywords that showed your ads in Search In-Grid placements and delivered high performance. Promote those into Manual campaigns.
- **Manual campaigns:** Bid higher on the keywords your best items convert on; remove the underperformers.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_SP_ITEM_KEYWORD`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="WMT_KEYWORD — Keyword-level performance (Manual campaigns only)">

Available in Manual / Keyword-bidding campaigns only. Includes two columns that don't appear in `WMT_SP_ITEM_KEYWORD`:

- **Bid** — the maximum you'll pay for a click when this target triggers your ad.
- **Average CPC** — the average amount you actually paid for a click.

The `searched_keyword` column tells you where your ad showed:

- If alphabetical or alphanumeric → your ad appeared on the Search In-Grid position for that keyword.
- If numeric → your ad appeared on the <Term>Buy Box</Term> or Item Carousel for that item ID.

Data is included in the Keyword Performance report only if at least one of these is true for the row:

- Impressions > 10
- Ad spend > $0.00
- Attributed sales > $0.00

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_KEYWORD`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Where ads appeared — context and placement

Three reports tell you *where* your ads were served. Combined with item / keyword data above, they form a full picture of campaign reach.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="WMT_PAGE_TYPE — Page type performance">

Performance of the page types where your campaign's items appeared.

| Page type | What it means |
|---|---|
| `browse` | Sub-category pages with algorithmic item selection |
| `item` | Specific item pages |
| `search` | Search pages based on a customer's query |
| `topic` | Custom curated search pages reached from external searches |
| `category` | Category pages |
| `homepage` | Home-page high-visibility personalization carousels (**Auto Campaigns only**) |
| `other` | Pages our beacon couldn't track, or that don't fit any category above |

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_PAGE_TYPE`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="WMT_PLACEMENT — Placement performance">

Performance broken down by where the ad was served on the page. Use placement bid multipliers to tilt spend toward the placements that convert best.

| Campaign type | Available placements |
|---|---|
| Keyword campaign | `Search In-grid`, `Carousel`, `Buybox`, `Others` |
| Auto campaign | `Search In-grid`, `Carousel`, `Buybox`, `Browse In-Grid`, `Stock Up`, `Home Page`, `Others` |

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_PLACEMENT`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="WMT_PLATFORM — Platform / device performance">

Performance broken down by where the customer saw your ad.

| Platform | What it means |
|---|---|
| `app` | The Walmart iOS and Android app |
| `desktop` | The Walmart.com website on desktop and tablet |
| `mobile` | The Walmart.com website on mobile web |
| `others` | Platforms we couldn't detect at the time |

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.WMT_PLATFORM`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Data freshness and history

- **Backfill at connection:** up to **90 days** of historical data is pulled the first time you connect.
- **Daily refresh:** new data lands every day.
- **15-day reconciliation pass:** Walmart finalizes reports on a 14-day lag. DataHawk re-fetches each day's data 15 days after the first download so the consolidated numbers stick.
- **Ongoing storage:** every day's data is backed up so you can run trend analyses across the full history of your account.
