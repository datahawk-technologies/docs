Optimize your Amazon product listings with data-driven insights.

## What is Listing Quality Analysis?

DataHawk analyzes your Amazon listings daily and gives each product a **Listing Quality Score (LQS)** — a number from 0 to 100 that reflects how well your listing is optimized for visibility and conversion.

The score is built from 25+ measurable criteria across your title, images, bullet points, and description, evaluated against Amazon's official listing guidelines.

## What can you use it for?

**Find which products need urgent attention** — The LQS pinpoints underperforming listings by scoring the elements most tied to purchase behavior — so you know exactly where to focus first.

**Catch Amazon policy violations before they cost you** — The analysis flags content that could trigger a listing suppression or penalty, such as prohibited keywords, missing images, or pricing in the title.

**Benchmark against competitors** — Compare your LQS against competitor listings to prioritize products with the highest improvement potential.

## How the score is calculated

Your score is made up of four sections, each weighted by its impact on conversion:

| Section | Weight | What it looks at |
|---|---|---|
| Title | 40% | Length, capitalisation, brand inclusion, prohibited content |
| Media | 30% | Number of images, video presence, A+ content |
| Bullet points | 20% | Count, length, punctuation, capitalisation |
| Description | 10% | Length, A+ content inclusion |

**Title quality has the biggest impact on your score** — nearly half of your total LQS comes from the title alone. If you can only focus on one area, start there.

Within each section, individual rules are weighted by their importance according to Amazon's guidelines (on a scale of 1–5). A listing that meets all the high-importance rules will score significantly better than one that passes only minor criteria.

<Callout type="info">
  Scores are only calculated when sufficient listing data exists. New products or recently connected accounts may not have a score immediately.
</Callout>

## What good looks like

| Score range | What it means |
|---|---|
| 80–100 | Well-optimized listing — minor improvements only |
| 60–79 | Room for improvement — review flagged criteria |
| Below 60 | Significant issues — prioritize this listing |

## Tips for getting the most out of LQS

- Start with your lowest-scoring listings — these have the most to gain
- Re-check scores after making changes, as they update daily
- Benchmark against your top competitors to understand the gap
- Combine with sales data: a low-converting product with a low LQS is your highest-priority fix

|---|---|---|---|
| `PRODUCT_LQA_CRITERION` | Raw listing metrics | Daily | product_key, criterion_key, date |

### Interpreting Null vs Empty Values

- **Null value** — DataHawk could not extract this data (unknown state)
- **Empty value** — Confirmed absence of this characteristic (e.g. an empty "Emojis in title" field means no emojis were found)

### Score Calculation Formula

```
Section score = sum(rule_compliances × rule_weights) / max_section_points
Global LQS = (Title Score × 0.4) + (Media Score × 0.3) + (Bullets Score × 0.2) + (Description Score × 0.1)
```
