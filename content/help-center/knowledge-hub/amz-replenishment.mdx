Amazon's Subscribe & Save (<Term>SnS</Term>) program lets customers subscribe to recurring deliveries of a product at a discount. For sellers, it creates predictable recurring revenue — but it is highly sensitive to inventory availability. A stock-out doesn't just mean a missed sale; it means a missed subscription delivery, which can trigger cancellations and lost recurring revenue.

DataHawk provides two complementary datasets:

- **Performance** — what actually happened (historical data)
- **Forecast** — what Amazon expects will happen (future projections)

Both datasets work at the <Term>ASIN</Term> level, broken down by <Term>marketplace</Term> and time period.

## What you can answer with these datasets

**1. Which products are driving recurring revenue through Subscribe & Save**

See total subscription revenue and what share of your overall revenue comes from <Term>SnS</Term> subscriptions — by <Term>ASIN</Term>.

**2. Is my subscription base growing?**

Track the number of active subscriptions and shipped subscription units over time to spot trends.

**3. Which ASINs are at risk due to inventory issues?**

Monitor units not delivered due to out-of-stock and the estimated revenue lost as a result. This is the most operationally critical metric in the dataset.

**4. What should my inventory plan look like for the next 60–90 days?**

Use the Forecast dataset to see how many subscription units Amazon expects to ship in the coming weeks — so you can replenish before stock runs out.

**5. What subscription revenue should I expect next month or quarter?**

Access Amazon's projected subscription revenue for the next 30, 60, and 90 days per ASIN.

## How to use Performance and Forecast together

**Step 1 — Plan:** Use the Forecast dataset to estimate upcoming demand.

**Step 2 — Execute and monitor:** Use the Performance dataset to check what was actually shipped, what was missed due to out-of-stock, and what the revenue impact was.

**Step 3 — Act:** If you see high forecasted demand alongside high out-of-stock losses, you have an urgent replenishment gap. Address it before the next delivery cycle.

## Key metrics at a glance

### Performance metrics (what happened)

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Shipped Subscription Units | Units successfully delivered via SnS |
| Total Subscription Revenue | Revenue generated by SnS orders |
| Active Subscriptions | Number of active subscriptions for the ASIN |
| Not Delivered Due to Out of Stock | Subscription units that couldn't be delivered due to stock unavailability |
| Lost Revenue Due to Out of Stock | Estimated revenue lost from unfulfilled subscription deliveries |
| Revenue Penetration | Share of total revenue attributable to Subscribe & Save |
| Coupons Revenue Penetration | Share of SnS revenue attributed to coupon activity |

### Forecast metrics (what's expected)

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Next 30-day Subscription Revenue | Amazon's projected SnS revenue in the next 30 days |
| Next 60-day Subscription Revenue | Amazon's projected SnS revenue in the next 60 days |
| Next 90-day Subscription Revenue | Amazon's projected SnS revenue in the next 90 days |
| Next 60-day Shipped Subscription Units | Projected subscription units to ship in the next 60 days |
| Next 90-day Shipped Subscription Units | Projected subscription units to ship in the next 90 days |

## ⚙️ For Developers & Analysts

### Dataset names

| Dataset | What it contains |
|---|---|
| `FBA_RESPLENISHMENT_SNS_PERFORMANCE` | Historical Subscribe & Save performance data |
| `FBA_RESPLENISHMENT_SNS_FORECAST` | Amazon's forward-looking Subscribe & Save projections |

### Granularity

Both datasets follow the same grain: **1 row = 1 ASIN × 1 marketplace × 1 time interval (period)**

### Common fields available in both datasets

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| `ASIN` | Amazon Standard Identification Number |
| `MARKETPLACE_KEY` | Marketplace identifier (e.g. Amazon-US) |
| `PRODUCT_KEY` | DataHawk internal product identifier — use to join with other DataHawk tables |
| `TIME_INTERVAL_START_DATE` | Start of the period covered by the metrics |
| `TIME_INTERVAL_END_DATE` | End of the period covered by the metrics |
| `CURRENCY_CODE` | ISO currency code (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.) |
