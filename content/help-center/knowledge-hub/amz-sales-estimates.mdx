Sales Estimates give you a daily view of how many units any tracked product sells — including competitors you don't have access to.

<Callout type="info">
  **April 2026 update — Child-level estimates now available**

  DataHawk has always provided sales estimates at the **parent <Term>ASIN</Term> level** (combined sales across all variants). You can now also access **true child-level estimates** — individual sales figures per variant — available in the new `PRODUCT.PRODUCT_SALES_ESTIMATES` table alongside the parent totals.

  Existing parent-level datasets (`PRODUCT_SALES_ESTIMATES_V5`, `PRODUCT_SALES_ESTIMATES_V5_RAW`) remain fully available and unchanged.
</Callout>

## What Business Decisions Can You Make?

Combined with market share data, this powers decisions across three areas:

### Product research

- Identify high-demand, low-competition products before entering a category.
- Are there underserved niches worth entering?
- Which of your variants actually drives most of the family's sales?

### Competitor benchmarking

- Is a competitor gaining or losing ground in your category?
- Which brands dominate the top 100, and is that changing?
- What is your brand's share of total category volume?

### Inventory planning

- Use sales trend data to forecast demand and avoid stockouts or overstock situations.
- Is demand in this category seasonal, and by how much?

## Getting Started

To receive sales estimates, you need to track **at least one of the following:**

**Track a category** → DataHawk collects Best Sellers Rank (<Term>BSR</Term>) data and calculates daily estimates for all top 100 products in that category. This is the primary way to get competitor estimates.

**Track specific products** *(optional)* → Estimates for individual ASINs you add manually. History starts from the day you begin tracking.

**Create product segments** *(optional)* → Group products with [tags](/help-center/data-setup/tags) to compare your catalogue against a specific competitor set.

**Connect your Seller account** *(optional but recommended)* → Your actual sales data helps calibrate the model for your own products, improving estimate accuracy.

<Callout type="info">
  Once you track a category, DataHawk begins collecting and estimating daily. The more history accumulates, the more reliable trend analysis becomes.
</Callout>

## Capabilities Overview

### Product tracking vs. category tracking

| | Category Tracking | Product Tracking |
|---|---|---|
| **Coverage** | All top 100 products in the category, including competitors | Only the ASINs you explicitly track |
| **History** | Up to 2 years (from DataHawk collection start per marketplace) | From the day you start tracking |
| **Best for** | Market sizing, brand share, competitive landscape | Focused monitoring of a specific competitor set or your own catalog |

### Parent vs. child ASIN estimates

| Level | What it represents | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Parent** | Combined sales across all variations in the family | Market share analysis, brand-level comparisons, category sizing |
| **Child** | Estimated sales for each individual variant | Variant-level decisions, specific SKU planning, understanding which size or color drives sales |

| Accuracy rating | Marketplaces |

Estimates are approximations, not exact figures. Revenue estimates depend on the accuracy of captured price data. Sudden rank changes (flash sales, stockouts) may cause temporary spikes that the model smooths over subsequent days.

## How It Works

1. **Collect Best Sellers Rank data** — DataHawk monitors BSR for all products in tracked categories daily.
2. **Calibrate by category** — DataHawk trains a separate model per category, converting rank position into estimated daily units specific to that category's sales velocity.
3. **Adjust for sales events** — Daily adjustments to estimates during high-velocity periods (Prime Day, Black Friday, etc.).
4. **Distribute to child variants** — Parent-level estimates are distributed using direct purchase signals, 7-day rolling averages, or equal distribution when no signal exists.
5. **Fill gaps** — When BSR data is temporarily unavailable, DataHawk applies linear extrapolation. Extrapolated rows are flagged (`IS_EXTRAPOLATED = true`).

## Accuracy & Reliability

Each row in the dataset carries a `TRUST_INDEX` score indicating model confidence for that product and date. Rows where extrapolation was applied are flagged with `IS_EXTRAPOLATED`.

### FAQ

**I was already using parent-level estimates — do I need to change anything?**
No. The existing `PRODUCT_SALES_ESTIMATES_V5` tables are unchanged. The new `PRODUCT_SALES_ESTIMATES` table is an addition, not a replacement.

**Why are child-level estimates different from the parent total?**
Parent estimates represent total family sales. Child estimates distribute those sales across each variant. The sum of `UNITS_CHILD` across all variants should equal `UNITS_PARENT`.

**Can I get estimates for products I don't track directly?**
Yes — if a product appears in a tracked category's top 100, you'll automatically receive estimates for it.

**What is `IS_EXTRAPOLATED`?**
When BSR data is temporarily unavailable, DataHawk fills the gap using linear extrapolation. These rows are flagged so you can include or exclude them in your analysis.

**What is `TRUST_INDEX`?**
A per-row confidence score reflecting data quality for that product and date.
