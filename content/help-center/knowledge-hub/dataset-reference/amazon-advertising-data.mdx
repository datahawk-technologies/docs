This page is the reference for Amazon advertising data in DataHawk. Most performance tables live in the `ADVERTISING` schema, while account and portfolio lookup tables live in `REFERENTIAL`. Use this page to understand the main table families, then use the [Exhaustive Column Referential](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1gYGqKQP9Z71nz1lOb4y0wJ15i27NvsUzdliIJeIx0HY/edit#gid=175439716) for the current column-level reference.

<Callout type="info">
  To connect your Amazon Advertising account so these tables start populating, see [Amazon Private Data - Advertising Account](/help-center/data-setup/ads-account).
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
  Exact columns, data types, and descriptions live in the [Exhaustive Column Referential](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1gYGqKQP9Z71nz1lOb4y0wJ15i27NvsUzdliIJeIx0HY/edit#gid=175439716). This page explains which table family to use and what each family is for.
</Callout>

## Aggregated performance tables

Start here for most dashboarding and BI work. These tables aggregate Amazon advertising data into common analysis grains: account, campaign, keyword, product, and <Term>SKU</Term>.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="ADVERTISING_ACCOUNT_METRICS — Account-level performance">

Advertising performance aggregated by account, date, and sponsored type. Use it for high-level spend, sales, <Term>ACoS</Term>, <Term>ROAS</Term>, impressions, and clicks before drilling into campaigns or products.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_ACCOUNT_METRICS`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="ADVERTISING_CAMPAIGN_METRICS — Campaign performance">

Advertising performance aggregated by account, campaign, date, and sponsored type. Use it to compare campaigns, monitor budget allocation, and investigate which campaigns drive changes in overall performance.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_CAMPAIGN_METRICS`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="ADVERTISING_KEYWORD_METRICS — Keyword performance (recommended)">

Keyword-level advertising performance. Use `ADVERTISING_KEYWORD_METRICS` as the recommended table for new keyword-level analyses. `ADVERTISING_KEYWORD_METRICS_V2` remains available when you specifically need its V2 surface.

**Data tables:**

- `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_KEYWORD_METRICS`
- `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_KEYWORD_METRICS_V2`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Product and SKU metrics — Product-level ad performance">

Product-level advertising performance, with different grains depending on the question:

- Use `ADVERTISING_PRODUCT_METRICS` for product performance by date.
- Use `ADVERTISING_PRODUCT_CAMPAIGN_METRICS` when you need product performance inside campaign context.
- Use `ADVERTISING_PRODUCT_SKU_METRICS` when SKU-level attribution is available and relevant.

**Data tables:**

- `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_PRODUCT_METRICS`
- `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_PRODUCT_CAMPAIGN_METRICS`
- `ADVERTISING.ADVERTISING_PRODUCT_SKU_METRICS`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

<Callout type="warn">
  Advertising tables can differ by grain: account, campaign, keyword, product, SKU, or date. Before joining tables or summing metrics, confirm the grain in [Find Tables and Columns](/help-center/knowledge-hub/dataset-reference/find-tables-and-columns).
</Callout>

## Sponsored Products source reports

These tables stay close to Amazon Sponsored Products reporting surfaces. Use them when you need report-style data for reconciliation, audit work, or deeper optimization beyond the aggregated tables.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="AMZN_SP_ADVERTISED_PRODUCTS — Advertised product performance">

Performance data for products advertised as part of Sponsored Products campaigns. Use it to analyze advertised <Term>ASIN</Term> performance and compare product-level results across campaigns.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_ADVERTISED_PRODUCTS`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="AMZN_SP_CAMPAIGN_BY_* — Campaign reporting">

Sponsored Products campaign reports with different grouping levels. Use the campaign-level table for standard campaign reporting, the ad-group table for more granular campaign structure, and the placement table when placement performance matters.

**Data tables:**

- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_CAMPAIGN_BY_CAMPAIGN`
- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_CAMPAIGN_BY_AD_GROUP`
- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_CAMPAIGN_BY_CAMPAIGN_PLACEMENT`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="AMZN_SP_SEARCH_TERM and AMZN_SP_TARGETING — Query and target performance">

Use search-term data to understand what customers actually typed or matched against, and targeting data to evaluate the keyword or product targets you configured in Amazon Ads.

**Data tables:**

- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_SEARCH_TERM`
- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_TARGETING`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="AMZN_SP_PURCHASED_PRODUCTS — Purchased product attribution">

Sponsored Products purchased-product reporting shows products that were purchased after an ad interaction, including products that were not the advertised item. Use it to understand cross-ASIN purchasing behavior and downstream attribution.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_PURCHASED_PRODUCTS`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="AMZN_SP_GROSS_AND_INVALID_TRAFFIC_REPORT — Traffic quality">

Gross and invalid traffic reporting for Sponsored Products. Use it when reconciling clicks, impressions, or traffic quality against Amazon Ads reports.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SP_GROSS_AND_INVALID_TRAFFIC_REPORT`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Display

Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Display data has its own campaign and report tables. Use these when you need campaign-type-specific analysis, especially brand-building metrics such as <Term>NTB</Term> where Amazon exposes them.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="Sponsored Brands tables">

Sponsored Brands tables cover campaign, search-term, and traffic-quality reporting. Use them for Sponsored Brands campaign analysis, search-term review, and invalid-traffic reconciliation.

**Data tables:**

- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SB_CAMPAIGNS`
- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SB_SEARCH_TERM`
- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SB_GROSS_AND_INVALID_TRAFFIC_REPORT`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Sponsored Display tables">

Sponsored Display tables cover campaign reporting and traffic-quality reporting. Use them for Sponsored Display performance analysis and reconciliation against Amazon's Sponsored Display reports.

**Data tables:**

- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SD_CAMPAIGNS`
- `ADVERTISING.AMZN_SD_GROSS_AND_INVALID_TRAFFIC_REPORT`

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Brand Store and DSP

Amazon Brand Store and <Term>DSP</Term> data sit alongside sponsored-ads performance, but they answer different questions: owned-store engagement and upper-funnel / programmatic advertising performance.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="AMZN_BRAND_STORE_METRICS — Brand Store performance">

Daily Brand Store performance metrics, including store sales, units, orders, views, and visits. Use it to understand how shoppers engage with your Amazon Store and how store activity changes over time.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.AMZN_BRAND_STORE_METRICS`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="AMZN_DSP_CAMPAIGNS — DSP campaign performance">

Amazon DSP campaign performance. Use it for DSP-specific analysis such as spend, sales, impressions, clicks, detail page views, and new-to-brand performance.

**Data table:** `ADVERTISING.AMZN_DSP_CAMPAIGNS`

<Callout type="info">
  DSP performance is not marketplace-specific. DataHawk no longer filters DSP data by the marketplace of the related advertising account.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Advertising referentials

Use these lookup tables to connect advertising facts to account, report, and portfolio metadata.

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="REFERENTIAL_ADVERTISING_ACCOUNT — Connected advertising accounts">

Lists advertising accounts connected to DataHawk and the account metadata used to populate the `ADVERTISING` schema.

**Data table:** `REFERENTIAL.REFERENTIAL_ADVERTISING_ACCOUNT`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="REFERENTIAL_ADVERTISING_ACCOUNT_REPORTS — Report availability">

Tracks report availability and status by advertising account. Use it when investigating whether a report is expected, missing, pending, or unavailable for an account.

**Data table:** `REFERENTIAL.REFERENTIAL_ADVERTISING_ACCOUNT_REPORTS`

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="REFERENTIAL_ADVERTISING_PORTFOLIO — Portfolio lookup">

Links portfolio IDs to portfolio names across advertising types. Use it to label campaign data with portfolio names.

**Data table:** `REFERENTIAL.REFERENTIAL_ADVERTISING_PORTFOLIO`

<Callout type="info">
  Portfolio names are updated every two weeks.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## Data freshness and history

- **Backfill at connection:** DataHawk retrieves up to **60 days** of Amazon Advertising history when you connect an account.
- **Daily refresh:** advertising data is updated daily.
- **Recent data can be incomplete:** the latest 24-48 hours may still be processing on Amazon's side or in DataHawk's sync.
- **API limitations apply:** some metrics, reports, campaign types, or marketplaces depend on what Amazon exposes through the Ads API.
- **Marketplace coverage can vary:** the Amazon Advertising setup page notes that some marketplaces are not supported for Sponsored Ads data because of Amazon API availability.

## Attribution and metric notes

Ad performance metrics use Amazon's 14-day post-click attribution window for sales, orders, and conversions. DSP RoAS uses Total Cost (not Ad Spend) and includes both view-through and click-through attribution within the same 14-day window.

For exact metric definitions in dashboards, see [Advertising Power BI Dashboard](/help-center/dashboard-templates/power-bi/advertising-dashboard).
