DataHawk gives you granular access to Amazon customer return records, covering both <Term>FBA</Term> (Fulfilled by Amazon) and <Term>FBM</Term> (Fulfilled by Merchant) orders. This data helps you understand not just how many products are coming back, but why — and what happens to them when they do.

## What you can analyze

- Which products are returned most frequently
- When returns peak and how patterns change over time
- Why customers are returning items (based on Amazon's reason codes)
- What condition returned items are in and whether they can be resold

## Understanding return dispositions (FBA)

When a customer returns an FBA item, Amazon inspects it and assigns a disposition:

| Disposition | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Sellable** | Item is in good condition and returned to your inventory |
| **Damaged** | Damaged by carrier or fulfillment center — Amazon reimburses you |
| **Customer Damaged** | Packaging opened or item altered by customer — not resellable, stays in inventory |
| **Defective** | Product is faulty or non-functional |
| **Expired** | Product is nearing its expiration date — may be removed or disposed of |

## Common return reasons

Amazon assigns a reason code to each return based on the customer's selection:

| Reason | What it signals |
|---|---|
| Unwanted item | Buyer's remorse — consider improving product images and descriptions |
| Not as described | Listing accuracy issue — review your content |
| Defective item | Product quality issue — investigate with your supplier |
| Wrong size / Wrong style | Variant confusion — consider clearer size guides or variant naming |
| Never arrived | Fulfillment or logistics issue |
| Found better price elsewhere | Pricing competitiveness issue |
| Damaged by carrier | Packaging issue — consider reinforcing packaging |

## ⚙️ For Developers & Analysts

### Available Data Tables

Returns data is available in the `SELLING_PARTNER` schema.

| Table | Coverage | Available since | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `RETURNS_DATA_SRC` | <Term>FBM</Term> (Fulfilled by Merchant) orders only | June 2023 | Return records keyed by return date. Includes <Term>ASIN</Term>, <Term>SKU</Term>, return quantities, reason codes, and metadata. |
| `FBA_CUSTOMER_RETURNS_SRC` | FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) orders | — | Detailed FBA return records including Fulfillment Center ID, disposition, customer comments, and reason codes. |

### Joining with Order Data

To enrich returns with order-level context, join against `finance.finance_orders`:

```sql
SELECT
  o.fulfillment_channel,
  r.*
FROM SELLING_PARTNER.RETURNS_DATA_SRC r
LEFT OUTER JOIN finance.finance_orders o
  ON o.order_id = r.order_id
```
