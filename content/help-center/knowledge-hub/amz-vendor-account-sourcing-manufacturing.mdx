This document explains the difference between **Sourcing** and **Manufacturing** in Amazon Vendor Central, and how this impacts the data you see in DataHawk dashboards.

## The Difference Between Sourcing and Manufacturing

### Sourcing

Sourcing refers to products that **Amazon purchases directly from your vendor account**.

- You act as the **supplier to Amazon**
- Data reflects only what **you sold to Amazon**

This unlocks the Sourcing view of Amazon allowing you to see **your own contribution to sales only.**

### Manufacturing

Manufacturing refers to products that **belong to your brand (you are the manufacturer)**.

- You are the **producer/brand owner of the product**
- Amazon may buy the product from you or from other vendors

This unlocks the Manufacturing view of Amazon allowing you to see the **total performance of your product on Amazon**, regardless of who supplied it.

## Why this matters

Amazon provides different "views" of your data depending on **how products are sourced**. **These views determine what you see in Vendor Analytics and in DataHawk**.

## What data is available?

### If your product is in Sourcing only

You will only be able to see:

- Sales data
- Inventory data

But you will **not** see:

- Traffic data (e.g., Glance Views)
- Forecasting data
- Some order-related metrics

### If your product is in Manufacturing

You will see all the data for **the total product performance on Amazon** — whether you directly sold to Amazon or not with your Vendor account. You have access to much more data, such as **Sales, Inventory, Traffic (Glance Views), Orders, and Forecasting.**

## Impact on dashboards (DataHawk)

If only Sourcing data is available:

- Some products may **not appear** in certain portions of the dashboards
- Reports using traffic data may show **no results**

This does not indicate a data issue — it reflects the data provided by Amazon.
