DataHawk gives you several ways to explore your profitability — from a high-level account view down to individual brands and SKUs. This page explains where to find what, and covers a common point of confusion around how Amazon fees appear in reports.

## Where to explore profit in DataHawk

| What you want to see | Where to find it |
|---|---|
| Profitability by <Term>SKU</Term> or <Term>ASIN</Term> | **Profit Explorer** |
| Brand → <Term>SKU</Term> breakdown with fees and ad spend | **Download Center** |
| Account-level fees (storage, etc.) | "No Brand" / "No SKU" row in Download Center |

<Callout type="warn">
  Organic vs. paid sales breakdown is not currently available in the Download Center view.
</Callout>

## Why some fees don't appear at the SKU level

This is one of the most common questions about profit reporting — and the answer lies in how Amazon itself reports fees.

Amazon charges fees in two different ways:

**SKU-level fees** — fees Amazon attributes directly to a specific product (e.g. <Term>FBA</Term> fulfillment fees per unit shipped). These appear at the SKU level in DataHawk, so you can see exactly what each product costs to fulfill.

**Account-level fees** — fees Amazon bills at the account level without breaking them down by product (e.g. <Term>FBA</Term> storage fees, long-term storage fees). Amazon does not tell DataHawk which SKU these belong to, so DataHawk cannot automatically split them.

In the **Download Center**, account-level fees appear grouped under **"No Brand" / "No SKU"**. They are not missing from your P&L — they are fully accounted for at the account level, just not distributed across individual products.

## What if I need storage fees allocated by SKU?

It's possible, but it requires defining a custom allocation rule — for example, distributing fees proportionally by unit volume, revenue share, or storage space used. This is not available out of the box.

Reach out to your DataHawk representative to discuss options.
