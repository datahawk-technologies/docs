---
title: Subscribe & Save Performance and Forecast
description: Understand DataHawk's Subscribe & Save performance and forecast datasets, key metrics, and inventory-planning use cases.
---

Amazon Subscribe & Save lets customers subscribe to recurring deliveries of a product at a discount. For sellers, it can create predictable recurring revenue, but it is highly sensitive to inventory availability. A stockout does not only mean a missed sale; it can mean a missed subscription delivery, which can trigger cancellations and lost recurring revenue.

DataHawk separates Subscribe & Save into two complementary views:

| View | What it tells you | How to use it |
|---|---|---|
| **Performance** | What actually happened in past subscription periods | Measure shipped units, subscription revenue, active subscriptions, and missed deliveries |
| **Forecast** | What Amazon expects will happen in upcoming periods | Plan inventory before future subscription deliveries are missed |

Both views work at the <Term>ASIN</Term> level, broken down by <Term>marketplace</Term> and time period.

## What you can answer

<Accordions>

<Accordion title="Which products are driving recurring revenue through Subscribe & Save?">
  Use subscription revenue and revenue penetration to identify products where recurring orders are becoming a meaningful part of the business.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is my subscription base growing?">
  Track active subscriptions and shipped subscription units over time to see whether recurring demand is expanding, stable, or declining.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Which ASINs are at risk due to inventory issues?">
  Monitor units not delivered due to out-of-stock and the estimated revenue lost as a result. This is usually the most operationally urgent signal in the dataset.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What should my inventory plan look like for the next 60-90 days?">
  Use forecasted subscription units and revenue to understand upcoming demand before the delivery window arrives.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What subscription revenue should I expect next month or quarter?">
  Use Amazon's projected subscription revenue for the next 30, 60, and 90 days per ASIN.
</Accordion>

</Accordions>

## How to use Performance and Forecast together

<Steps>

<Step>

### Plan with Forecast

Use the Forecast view to estimate upcoming subscription demand by ASIN, marketplace, and period.

</Step>

<Step>

### Monitor with Performance

Use the Performance view to check what was actually shipped, what was missed due to out-of-stock, and what revenue impact followed.

</Step>

<Step>

### Act on the gap

If you see high forecasted demand alongside high out-of-stock losses, you have an urgent replenishment gap. Address it before the next delivery cycle.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Key metrics at a glance

### Performance metrics

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Shipped Subscription Units | Units successfully delivered through Subscribe & Save |
| Total Subscription Revenue | Revenue generated by Subscribe & Save orders |
| Active Subscriptions | Number of active subscriptions for the ASIN |
| Not Delivered Due to Out of Stock | Subscription units that could not be delivered because inventory was unavailable |
| Lost Revenue Due to Out of Stock | Estimated revenue lost from unfulfilled subscription deliveries |
| Revenue Penetration | Share of total revenue attributable to Subscribe & Save |
| Coupons Revenue Penetration | Share of Subscribe & Save revenue attributed to coupon activity |

### Forecast metrics

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Next 30-day Subscription Revenue | Amazon's projected subscription revenue in the next 30 days |
| Next 60-day Subscription Revenue | Amazon's projected subscription revenue in the next 60 days |
| Next 90-day Subscription Revenue | Amazon's projected subscription revenue in the next 90 days |
| Next 60-day Shipped Subscription Units | Projected subscription units to ship in the next 60 days |
| Next 90-day Shipped Subscription Units | Projected subscription units to ship in the next 90 days |

## Interpretation notes

- Forecast metrics are Amazon projections, not guaranteed demand.
- Out-of-stock losses are especially important for Subscribe & Save because a missed subscription delivery can weaken recurring demand.
- Compare Forecast and Performance together; either view alone can be misleading.
- Use inventory datasets alongside Subscribe & Save data when you need to understand whether missed deliveries came from availability, replenishment timing, or broader stock planning issues.
