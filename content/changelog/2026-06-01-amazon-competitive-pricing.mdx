A new dataset surfaces Amazon's competitive pricing benchmarks.

- **Data Sharing Path:** `PRODUCT.AMZN_PRODUCT_COMPETITIVE_PRICES`
- **Granularity:** Daily / <Term>ASIN</Term>
- **Source:** <Term>SP-API</Term>, Product Pricing v2022-05-01, `getCompetitiveSummary`. Collected for any product, not just owned.

**Key fields:**

- `Competitive Price`: Amazon's benchmark price for competitive positioning. Reflects what Amazon considers the current legitimate going rate for the product in new condition across eligible sellers.
- `Was Price`: The pre-promotion baseline Amazon uses for strikethrough pricing (e.g. "Was $18.49"). Reflects the price that appeared on the detail page before a discount was applied, derived from the product's own pricing history.

**Collection:**

- Snapshot report (no backfilling)
- Quarterly updates for almost all product sources (any product connected to DataHawk will be quarterly enriched)
- Daily updates for tracked products and seller listings (when enabled on the <Term>workspace</Term>)

Additional fields like `referencePrices`, `featuredBuyingOptions`, `lowestPricedOffers`, and `similarItems` are collected and may be exposed in the future.
